Quote from: Bangers on May 01, 2009, 04:34:12 pm
I think the $59 Deal from the Devil is the cheaper option, the Guinea pigs were the Cuperteens, who seem to be having some serious issues thus far. Electrons from the left coast are notoriously laid back.

Cuperteens testers/ advertisers? have not had ANY cdi problems when putting these on Puchs. I got one from benji, threw it on my magnum and it went really really really really fast. I should have GPS'd it, but I'd wager 55-58 on 18x40 (za50). I was doing maybe 45 before CDI. I managed to blow a 1st gear clutch in under 5 miles because it ripped so hard. In LA it was the 4th fastest bike behind Shaw, another cuperteen and a creature, but it was probably spinning higher rpms than all them except shaw. My CDI testing companions have had equally good luck. One put it on his ripper pinto which has always done 42 (super low end polini), and it had more torque, and did nearly 46, getting there much faster. For the weekend I decided I wanted to try it on my MagnumX. It was doing 37 with points. I bolted the CDI on and got a bit better mid-range and maybe 2 miles an hour up top. Our only issues have come in hacking stators, testing boxes not designed for the stator and attempting to transplant the system onto other motors. I'm not sure what you mean when you say west coast electronics are laid back when we build consistently the fastest, and most reliable bikes in the states. With this CDI system the playing field will be a bit more level, but more importantly, we can all have consistent, powerful spark so that tuning variable can be partially removed. While setting points is a menial task, perfecting your timing for MAXIMUM power is quite difficult, and cannot typically be done to a spec.

Regardless, the CDI is awesome, and an amazing deal for the reliability and power it will get you.
