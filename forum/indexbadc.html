« on: February 21, 2010, 09:16:13 pm »
So I was messing around in the shop today trying to fix one of my Batavus Regencys that has been having issues when I saw Mikes 80cc Arisol kit lying in an open box.  Out of curiosity I tried to put it on my spare m56 and HOLLY SHIT it fit.  The stud spacing is almost identical and with a bit of case shaving it would fit perfectly.  Now here is the real question, are the strokes the same, or even close.  I have no idea what Puch strokes or Batavus m56 strokes are, but I would need to find that out before proceeding. 

Problem 2.  The m56 is reed valve and case inducted and Puch kits are not.  So would it work if I sealed off the Batavus intake and use the Kits? This would be nice because it would mean that I could eailsy up grade the carb with out having to build another ridiculous intake to get around the m56 drive belt. 

Problem 3.  Batavi have dry clutches.  So is it even worth it to try and kit an m56, because I will just fry my clutch.

Yeah, you can mill the difference off the top of the cylinder. Be VERY careful about the nikasil flaking off, though. That's a real problem. This was almost an issue when I milled my DMP, and that was only about 30 thousandths with just a regular fly cutter.

Yeah, that'd work, but I'd make sure the intake duration was correct with the reduced stroke. Honestly though, I'd leave the reeds in. Since you're considering going through that much work, it's not much more to make an intake. Just buy a sheet of thicker carbon reed paper (probably .40mm would be great for that cylinder if you ported it) and make new reeds to fit the stock block.

As for the clutch, either it'll blow up or it won't. There are a lot of VERY powerful bikes that use a (multi plate) dry clutch, and they do just fine.

But seriously, build this motor.

Quote from: Lindar on February 21, 2010, 10:07:11 pm
but for serious performance, I'd think you'd want something bigger.

Eh, it wouldn't be so bad, since it's on the case and not on the cylinder. I'd assume it's got vertical reeds (like on the Gila) instead of horizontal reeds (like stock Polini). That, and a 360 degree intake duration add up to lower intake velocities, so you wouldn't lose as much performance on a smaller carb. I honestly think that having a 5mm stroke difference would ruin the intake timings if you ran it as a piston port. But that's just an opinion.
