Title: Re: Le Toy II teardown
Post by: Holmes on December 25, 2009, 08:26:03 pm
Only if the motor is closer to the tire than the pivot.  The other option of mounting motor on frame gets into anti squat, and that is a huge beast to deal with on a bicycle conversion.

I haven't drawn up the new rear end, but the motor should be within 6" of the pivot, and about 16" from the axle.  So most of the weight will act sprung.

Title: Re: Le Toy II teardown
Post by: Holmes on January 09, 2010, 09:09:31 pm
So I got this thing running!  The STL boys saw it run briefly before I had to leave, and it still needed some driveline tweaking.  I went down to GA with nothing but time and parts and got it running solid!  Until I really cranked up the power, that is.   This thing runs like a raped ape, just ballistic acceleration for such a light vehicle.  I really need to tone down the throttle response, it is a bit too jumpy. 

Put some hard throttle on it after running it a bit, and the set screw walked around the shaft and dug a nice trench into the jackshaft.  Of course the shavings got buried under the gears and galled the sprockets to the shaft.  Gotta find my gear puller to get this sorted out.  Maybe a flat spot is a good idea on this thing. 


So this isn't the most stealth electric moped because of the gearbox noise, but this is certainly the most powerful one I have ridden.  Under even "light" throttle I was hitting 5hp peaks.  I am told this motor will burst output in excess of 9hp with the right gearing.  Not bad for a 32oz motor.  I also have a double stack on the way, twice the motor power of the one I currently have.
