the woodruff key is a little D shaped piece of metal that goes into a slot on the crankshaft, and into a slot in the flywheel. See, on inside of the flywheel there's a lobe, the lobe opens the points,,, so the flywheel and the crankshaft need to be aligned so that happens at approximately TDC.   The Stator, the plate that the points condensor,and induction coils are mounted to , is itself mounted on slotted holes , so that it can be rotated at little , one way and the other,,,that's your timing adjustment.  retard and advance.
   If you have a strong spark, and its sparking at close to TDC, even without a carb, just a little spritz of gas should lite it up.
  compression can be an issue, but to start with, if you hold your finger over the spar plug hole, and feel anything,, you probably have enough compression to get it to run..
   so take a little stick, and stick in the spark plug hole, and rotate the motor till thats at the very top...take a felt tip, and make a mark from the flywheel onto the motor...then with a flshlignt, look thru the slots, turn the motor in the direction ti runs,,,and watch for those points to just start to open,,,,you should be within a 1/4 inch on the flywheel.....post your results.

And here's a picture of the woodruff key all by itself:



Basically, when the woodruff key is still on the flywheel, it's just slightly raised over the normal taper of the crankshaft. Then there's a notch in the inside hole of the flywheel like so:



The raised part of the woodruff key fits into that slot. This makes sure that your flywheel lines up on on the crankshaft the same all the time. If you're missing the key, your flywheel could be in a totally wrong position.

Durr. I already forgot we were talking about a ZA50. Yeah, I've got zero clue about those. I needed to take that flywheel off anyway though, so it's just as well There is some ZA50 timing stuff on pages 109-111 ish in the Puch service manual, but it's all the usual manual-version timing crap, with lots of special tools that nobody has or understands how to use or can afford (and by "nobody" I mean "me"). Someone here has gotta know about the woodruff key though.

One time someone told me to find TDC this way by using a piston stop: Rotate the flywheel until the piston hits the piston stop. Make a mark on the flywheel. Rotate the flywheel the other way until the piston hits the piston stop again. Make another mark on the flywheel (both marks referenced to the same point on the case ... I think). Then measure between those two marks -- the halfway point should be TDC. If you don't have a piston stop, you can make one with an old spark plug and some nuts and bolts (like <a href="http://www.mopedarmy.com/forums/discuss/read.php?f=7&i=75752&t=75554">this</a> -- it's a timing tool, but if you get the right hardware on it, it works as a piston stop too). It is probably better to try and avoid removing the flywheel unless you have to take it off, but if you ever do want to remove it, you can just stuff some rope into the spark plug hole. That will block off part of your cylinder and stop the piston from moving, which will also keep your flywheel from turning so you can get the nut off.
