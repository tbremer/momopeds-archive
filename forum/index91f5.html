Repair and Performance / Re: HONDA EXPRESS kick start issues
« on: April 30, 2012, 01:07:53 am »
If it's an assembly issue, you got to take it back apart and do it right. If it's the spring having snapped as they sometimes do, you will first gather protective garb. Padded in front, a face shield, and leather gloves. I had one of these things come out during the rewind process when my GF decided to bother me. ( I recently told her to please consider the next time she felt the urge to phone me, that I usually have my hands busy with my d**k or dynamite or something else very serious.) It struck me in the chest and left a nasty bruise. They usually break at one end or the other, if it is broken in the middle you are es-screwed. Use a propane torch to heat a section just back from the broken end to a nice orange and bend it to recreate the broken off section. Then don the protective gear and get it back into place. Keep your face and anything you value as far from the spring as possible. It will fight you like a honeybadger, be persistent, using screwdrivers and whatever you can think of. It will go in. If put in too tight the lever will not have enough action, if put in too loose the lever will not impart enough energy to turn the motor over.

Repair and Performance / Re: za50 clutch fixin question
« on: April 06, 2012, 09:09:47 am »
The pucks are way down in there. Much further than a wear item should be in my opinion. They are in fact underneath everything. You will need some serious free space to lay everything out in order with special care taken in regards to the shims. The new pucks will appear to not fit during the first few tries, but must be finessed in without harming them. Also if it was run without the pucks in good condition, there will be teeth missing on the gears.
