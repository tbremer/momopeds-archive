Have you changed to premium fuel?  I wonder what the end result compression ratio is.   When an engine exceeds a certain compression ratio, you need to upgrade the fuel to premium or you can have pre-ignition or "engine knock."  I always thought of 9.5:1 as requiring regular gas (what's that? 97 octane?) and 10.5:1 as needing premium, but I don't know the cutoff between those two.

Did the high-compression head say what the compression ratio would end up having?  I wonder how one would figure it. 

First, you can figure displacement mathematically with the cylinder dimensions of bore and stroke.  Or you could figure it by connecting a hose to the spark plug hole and run that hose to a graduated (measurement markings on the side) glass container held upside down under water.  Then, you would run the piston from bottom dead center to top dead center and measure the air pumped into the glass.  But that does not take into account the space above the piston in the head.

Second, I wonder how accurate a measurement you would get of just the head by removing it, holding it upside down and filling it with water.  Then dump out and measure the water.  You would then need to determine if the piston makes it all the way to the top of the cylinder.  If not, you must add the volume of the space from the top of the stroke to the bottom of the head. 

The true compression ratio would be the total volume from the first measurement plus the second compared to the second only.  You would call the second measurement "one" and see how many times the second measurement goes into the first.
