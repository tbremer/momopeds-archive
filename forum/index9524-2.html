Hi Dave,

Thanks so much for your ideas. Actually, I was going to do just that with the duct tape. I had also wanted to ask if anybody had different sized jets, because themopedjunkyard.com makes a big point about not adding a bigger jet to this carb. I think it recommends a #51, though I haven't taken mine apart to see what's in it since I learned the nomenclature.

In answer to your question, the later A3s had this carb, and the earlier ones had a different one. This is definitely the right carb for my moped. By the way, if anybody's looking for a performance carb, there's a guy on ebay who sells them and all kinds of other parts, and he's willing to deal if you offer him less than he's asking. For instance, he was asking $70 + shipping for a performance carb for my moped, and was willing to take $62 for it incl. shipping. Not bad. However, I'm not going to get it because according to mopedjunkyard, you can't upgrade the carb unless you upgrade the cylinder and intake on the A3.

I'll try the tape on the air intake. Anybody have some dellorto jets I could try out? With a bigger jet, it stands to reason I might get more power, since I have enough air. But cutting back the air seems like it would give me less power. Or does that make no sense? Still lots to learn.

And I need to get some new spark plugs so I can try out your test, Dave. However, my one concern is that when using gas/oil mixture, the spark plug color may not be eggshellwhite, since (at least on my plugs) it's always been somewhat oily and brownish colored.
