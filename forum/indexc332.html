 IF it is a clutch problem,,,it could be just the fluid level,,, they use automatic transmission fluid.
  I'll spare you the first rite of passage on the Sach motors.
 there's a slotted screw, on top of the motor, just above the cover plate, that says SACHS on it,, if you remove that,,, pieces will fall apart inside,,, and then you'll have to take the cover plate off, and try to figure out how they go back together,,,,,
 DON"T DO IT,,,,your welcome
  the clutch is an arrangement of disc and plates, that when squeezed together,,drive the chain.
  When you pull on the starter lever,, it squeezes these together, to make the motor start,,, outer side of the clutch
  As you rev the motor,, theres a doughnut shaped spring, that expands thru centrifugal force , that squeezes the discs and plates, to make it go,,,inner side of the clutch
   To start,,, your problem, may be low fluid level
  That's checked , by removing the plastic cap ,on  the side of the cover plate (there's also a drain plug underneath,,,_
  The fluid level is full, when it run out that hole,,with the bike level
  so tipping the bike one way, and the other,,, check and top of the level,,, if it was really empty,,, try running it,,,
   if the fluid level was fine,,, and nice clean fluid runs out,,, you may have to take that side cover off ,, for  ,,,well,,, you'll see,,,,
