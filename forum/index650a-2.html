Here's an online source for the essay it comes from...

http://www.emersoncentral.com/selfreliance.htm

A few translations, some metaphorical, varying in trueness to Emerson's message:


"Stretch your arm no farther than your sleeve will reach"
"Cut your Coat according to your Cloth"
"and do not look further than yourself"

"deproduction: literally, it means "don't seek yourself outside". The message is, you find yourself by looking inwards, not by turning to teachers and preachers and judgements of others."

"From the poet Persius, who lived in the first century AD: "... nec te quaesiveris extra". It is the last part of a sentence which is, I think, advice to a writer that if he finds that ignorant people do not understand him, he should take no notice, and simply stick to his own judgement."


Here is the quote from Emerson's essay I like best:

"Insist on yourself; never imitate. Your own gift you can present every moment with the cumulative force of a whole life's cultivation; but of the adopted talent of another, you have only an extemporaneous, half possession. That which each can do best, none but his Maker can teach him. No man yet knows what it is, nor can, till that person has exhibited it."
