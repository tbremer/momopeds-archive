I pwned a cop today
« on: February 10, 2010, 05:16:46 pm »
Okay, okay, "pwned" is a little strong. "Educated" is a nicer word...

Anyway, I was leaving work on west campus to go back to class, and not 50 feet down the road, there is a cop about to pull out of a parking lot. I was ahead of her, but since I saw her, I knew I had to behave more than I usually do. So I rode all the way back to main campus, signaling and coming to complete stops, and she FOLLOWED ME THE WHOLE WAY. I parked at Lindley where my class was, locked up, turned around, and saw she had stopped on the road. <sarcasm>Gee, I wonder what she could possibly be doing there?</sarcasm> So she gets my attention and says, "Are you supposed to have a brake light on that? I mean, I noticed that you didn't have one, or it wasn't working, so I'm just wondering ...." And I tell her that no, I don't need one, although that one does technically have one that doesn't work no matter what I do to it and OH WAIT HERE LET ME SHOW YOU THE COPY OF THE LAWS I PRINTED OFF THAT I CONVENIENTLY HAVE IN MY BACKPACK. So I showed her that only motorcycles and motor-driven cycles are required to have brake lights ("stop lights"), and that motorized bicycles are specifically exempted from the definition of a motorcycle or a motor-driven cycle. Then I told her how old the moped was and tried to make it sound as pathetic and feeble as possible so if she ever saw me again she would feel sorry for me and forgive whatever I was doing wrong. Then she babbled some more about how "she was just wondering, because you know, you didn't have one, and she wasn't sure if it was working, or if it was supposed to have, one .." and then I went to class -- smiling, because I totally pwned her. Next time I'll offer to give the cop my copy of the laws and suggest that they distribute it amongst their colleagues.

Couldn't you just have fought the ticket in court?

Also, MO has the same insurance requirements that KS does.

Under the Missouri Motor Vehicle Financial Responsibility Law (chapter 303), the law (section 303.025) says " No owner of a <b>motor vehicle</b> registered in this state, <b>or required to be registered in this state</b>, shall operate, register or maintain registration of a motor vehicle, or permit another person to operate such vehicle, unless the owner maintains the financial responsibility which conforms to the requirements of the laws of this state."

However, the definitions section of that law (section 303.020) states "(5) "Motor vehicle", a self-propelled vehicle which is designed for use upon a highway, <b>except</b> trailers designed for use with such vehicles, traction engines, road rollers, farm tractors, tractor cranes, power shovels, well drillers and <b>motorized bicycles</b>, as defined in section 307.180, RSMo, and every vehicle which is propelled by electric power obtained from overhead wires but not operated upon rails"

Now, if they catch you going over 30, and they know the laws and know that that means you're not technically a "motorized bicycle" anymore, then you might have problems. But so long as the cop isn't smart enough to connect all those dots, this should be good enough.

Chapter 303: //www.moga.mo.gov/STATUTES/C303.HTM
Definitions: //www.moga.mo.gov/statutes/C300-399/3030000020.HTM
Law requiring insurance: //www.moga.mo.gov/statutes/C300-399/3030000025.HTM

Disclaimer: There might be other laws in there that I haven't looked at that would negate all of this, but you'd have to dig and find them yourself.

Side note: The last clause on the motorized bicycle definition says "A motorized bicycle shall be considered a motor vehicle for purposes of any homeowners' or renters' insurance policy." So does that mean that if you have homeowners/renter's insurance, that covers you in some form?
