Strongbow.  You can get it in the states.

I am pretty easy going on the cider.  6 gallon carboy, let it ferment until it slows, then rack it into another carboy for another few days until a good layer forms on the bottom.   I really want to build a bottom siphon carboy to prevent this reracking.   Then I just bottle it and let it sit.  Mad carbonation with champagne yeast, even after just a few weeks in the bottle. Don't open them warm or it will spew out!  I have never added priming sugars, which indicates I am bottling before full fermentation has happened.   I do get particulates settling in the bottles, but it has never affected taste enough to warrant another racking.   I am more concerned over taste than looks anyway. 



I don't recommend adding other juices to it.  I have tried all kinds of combinations like cranberry and such.  Never turns out as tasty as expected, and always gets some strange estery flavors.

If you want to be super lazy, just pour out a bit of the cider, pitch the yeast,  and pop a cork and one way valve into the jar/ jug.  I just remembered that is how I did it last time and it rocked!  It seemed to keep some off flavors out as well, since pouring the cider into a big container will aerate it and can pull in nasties during the process.
