Honda Cub rebuild
« on: February 13, 2012, 10:54:49 pm »
Jared was so kind as to give away an old Cub 50 (or super cub, or something 50) that he bought years back and didn't fix up.  Alls I wanted was a frame to start with, and so RK and I stripped the bike down and had our way with it!   I seem to have a lot of parts for the honda 50s from back in my days of pit bikes, so I started collecting them together in anticipation of the build.  For the most part it will be a bolt and build bike!

The plans for the bike will be daily driver and back up bike at rallies.   I will probably have a wheelset with dual sports for going to the farm, I will be lacing my own wheels anyway so why not make an extra set?

The old bike didn't look so hot.  I forgot to take pics of it, but if you have seen a rusty honda 50 you have seen them all.  Both wheels were seized.  The gas tank wasn't so rusty though!    Original engine seems to have compression, but I don't need it.  All I want is the svelt looking frame!

So now we can start the "hardly anything bought" build.  I seriously had everything laying around!



Some sad panda wheels...





Poor pile of parts, who is going to look after you?







This is what happens to pit bike wheels in my house!  Hiyaaa!







Ok, so I didn't totally karate chop the hub into submission.  I did relace it into a 17" rim I had laying around.   Huzza for reusing parts!





Looking there, the pit bike hub works with the stock rear end!  Add a little magic dust for the disc brake and we are in action!







Lets just bolt up these pit bike shocks and see what happens.  Lookie there, same headset and everything!  I may have to thin the headtube, but that is hardly trouble.







I don't think she looks too bad so far!  I will relace the front hub into a 17" rim in time, but that will require redoing the shocks so my steering geometry doesn't get screwed up.
