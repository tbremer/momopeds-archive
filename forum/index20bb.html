If you have air leaks, it messes up the FUEL part. Changes the mixture, makes it leaner because it's introducing more air into a system that is supposed to be closed. Eventually you will overheat and seize.

Leaks at the pipe are different. Exhaust pipes can be thought of as a big heat sink, designed to carry heat away from the motor. When there's a leak at the header, heat can't escape through the pipe and eventually you will probably overheat and seize.

I've never seen an exhaust leak stop a motor from running though.
