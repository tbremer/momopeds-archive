I've always wanted to do a perry or fuck with pears... When it's done I'll trade you some bottles...

I'd just blend/juice the pears and dump it in secondary. Keep it simple and just rack carefully off the sediment into a third vessel before you bottle.

As far as yeast I've always use Wyeast's dry mead yeast but I think champagne yeast would be fine. Meads are nutrient poor so think about yeast nutrients. I have used DAP (I think, it's been a while...).  Make a starter to keep lag times down.

Never messed with acid blend but that would prolly be good with pears... Maybe a dash of citric acid... I usually pick fruits that will give the acids I want in the final flavor profile.

Are you gonna ferment to stillness or keep it fizzy. I prefer still meads.

Read Ken Schram's: The compleat meadmaker.  His fruit advice is solid.

I wouldn't use pectins to clarify, pectins cause haze. They occur naturally in fruits. Heat denatures them and makes them insoluble.

With careful (and patient) racking you shouldn't need any clarifying agents. And I mean patient, meads are not like beers. I have left meads in primary for 4-6 monthes and secondary for up to a year. Any time you think about racking, just take a step back and go do something else instead (like fixing yr brakes).

Plus a little sediment in the bottle is probably unavoidable. Just pour carefully.

If you gonna use fruit, the honey varietal will prolly get buried in the flavor mix.

I'd stay away from corn sugars too. it will wreck the texture, thin it out.

Do the cider Tom, and ask questions if you have some. I've done a few ciders and love 'em. If you're aiming for 12% that's more of an apple wine, but still should be really tasty. I'm assuming you're going for still? I always wrestle with carbonation in ciders. In the past I've always used brewers yeasts, so the %alc. was lower (I think 8% was the highest) and when it comes to carbonation you can either use apple juice concentrate or corn sugar. The issue is getting them to ferment dry and then priming them correctly. Try and get nice cider from an apple orchard, avoid anything with potassium metasulfate, potassium bicarbonate, etc. It will kill your yeast. Best bet is to either get a blended cider or to find ciders made from single apple varieties and blend them yourself. You might also need some acid blend, sometimes they're just not full bodied/acidic enough on their own.
