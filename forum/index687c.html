I'm not going to hold this against you because I don't think you're trying to be condescending, but just FYI, that does kind of come off as condescending. Not that some of what we're saying couldn't be taken as condescending too, though...

Anyway, I don't know enough about four strokes to make any kind of intelligent commentary on that (where is Justin? This thread needs more Justin), although I think we're all right here in that there are similarities and differences. At the very least, it sounds like you're saying that the combo package you put together didn't work because the parts didn't work together. Mopeds can be like that too -- some parts work really well with other parts, and some parts don't work as well with those other parts.

Back to the original question though. You say you've got an E50. That means that everything we've been saying so far applies, since we've been assuming the E50. We already kind of know what performance you're looking for, so now we just need to know how much money you want to throw at it. When you get it working, if there are things that seem really wrong with it (like loud clanking/grinding noises), those would probably be important to know too. You said in your original post that mostly the performance stuff seems geared towards bolt-on parts. In general, I think that's true. Rebuilding the bottom end is for reliability -- there isn't much speed to be gained there. The pipe, carb, and kit are all bolt-on, and they're all for speed. A CDI could potentially increase both speed and reliability. If you're looking for performance mods that aren't bolt-on, look into cylinder porting, milling the head (and squish bands and related topics), clutch tuning, or maybe building your own exhaust.

And I'm assuming you've already done/are planning to do this stuff, but there's also the "safety/replace old and gross stuff" category, which includes a new petcock/fuel line/filter, tires, tubes, cables, brakes, and <i>potentially</i> chain/sprockets, wiring, lights, and repacking the wheel bearings.

Oh, and as for the "won't engage the clutch when you pull the starter lever" problem... have you adjusted the cable? If it's already as tight as it goes, it might be time to look at the clutch and make sure the problem isn't there.
