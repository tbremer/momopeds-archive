Clicking/clanking from back tire brake area.
« on: November 28, 2011, 04:03:37 am »
77 Puch Maxi is making a clicking/clanking noise from the brake/bearing area of the rear wheel.

Recently repaired the chain tensioner and used 6oz slim in the rear wheel to fix a hole from a staple. Tire is aired up fine and holding air and my chain is nice and tight. Neither are rubbing on anything that I can see nor is anything else from visual inspection. The sound cannot be herd if you peddle the bike very slow but soon as you peddle with any speed the clanking starts and the faster you go the faster it goes. The sound continues till the back tire slows down to less then 1 mph with or without gas, coasting or peddles     
 
I can just put the bike on the kickstand and spin the back tire by hand and it is now frequently making the noise, it is a loud sound and it shakes the rim of the tire when it makes the sound. I have inspected the chain a few times and nothing seems stuck in it or messed up on the chain. nothing in the rims/spokes. The sound is coming from inside the brake compartment on the rear wheel.

So I have no clue. My guess is it could be the bearings going bad possibly from the weight of the green slime in the tire even the the bike rides fine? Or maybe something in the brake is rubbing. I just have no idea so I thought maybe someone could point me in the right direction. Thanks.

I highly doubt the bearings in your wheel went bad from the "weight of the green slime".  I do not trust that slime shit on a moped tube... it is just a band aid, you need to just replace the tube.  also, if your wheel is making crazy noises inspect your chain tension, inspect your sprockets for worn teeth, check for dragging brakes and if all looks good buy the proper size cone wrenches and take the wheel apart and re-pack the bearings (i'd just buy new wheel bearings) with fresh lithium grease (I use automotive bearing grease).

Take the back wheel off and clean everything and check assembly of all two parts. Your wheels take seconds to remove and you can actually see the components and make sure everything looks good. Nobody is going to be able to help you on the forum because nobody on the forum can see a single part much less adjust anything. It's good for you to start by taking stuff apart just for the sheer fact that it will help you learn how to fix something so simple as a clicking or wobbly wheel in your sleep. Post a bunch of pics of your progress if you want a hand.
