I kept debating about whether or not to post anything here just cause...
I mean this was my first rally. I have nothing to compare it to.

The pros outweigh the cons by like ten thousand.
Some of the MANY pros:
-The route, for sure. I don't know who came up with the original idea but it is SUPER FANTASTIC, whether you're an out of towner, or a native who never really pays attention to this stuff when you're stuck inside your car.
I forgot what the cathedral looked like!
-The map, oh god the map. I actually used it. I was basically not with the pack for..uh..75 percent of the ride, and hadn't paid much attention to the route until it was me, Bingo and Sam I Am saying "uh...where are we going?" Bingo busted out the map, issue resolved.
-The general representation of IRE. There were what, over 50 people there? IRE represented for sure, and I think that whether you knew members or not...by the end of it you knew exactly who everyone was. Everyone was super friendly and nice and I think it made a super boss impression on out of towners, yeah?
-Mark. YEAH, I KNOW IT'S REPETITIVE, BUT HELLO. Is there a Mark anywhere else? You are amazing. You are so nice, so helpful, always around, totally solved ALL OF MY "oh crap what am I riding" problems. I bet no other gang (oops, tribe) has ANYONE that can compare to you.
-Danielle. Again. Repetition but I think I love this woman. The hospitality, the kindness, and for someone like me...who doesn't quite feel  AS comfortable as everyone else..she just is really good about making one feel at home.
-Breakdown. Maybe it took a hot minute for Tom to get to me, but sweet jesus. What if something happened, if the route changed at the last minute, if there were no Tom, no breakdown, no nothing? In the end, my stuff got fixed (sorry I didn't fix it myself but c'mon guys...you gotta give me some time here), I continued to ride, and while it might not have been with the pack, it was still fun.
-The whole Royale thing. I'm really not a huge fan of the Royale (sorry, it's for mostly selfish pbr and no money reasons) but SS hooked us up! I mean we wound up paying five bucks and gots food and booze..having a "sponsor" (I guess that's what it was?) was super cool.
This whole thing can be pretty costly (especially for people who had to travel), and being able to eat and drink on the cheap (at a place that normally wouldn't be nearly as cheap) was pretty sweet.

Cons:
-There was that one minute where I was with the pack, and then it never happened again.
Since this was my first rally, it would've been pretty epic if I were able to stay with the group.
I don't think as many people have the same story that I do but basically I got stuck in Forest Park and never caught up again. Wait, I caught up @ the cement fac'try but then everyone diced out and I didn't really know what was happening. I think this complaint has been addressed in multiple posts, so I'm not even worried.


ugh this is all starting to sound like a broken record, sorry

I just want to say, that
at the end of the day
this was AWESOME
and if one of the goals in this was to get people to become more interested and active in the moped community,
MISSION ACCOMPLISHED AMIRITE.
