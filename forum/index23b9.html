I didn't label, but the rectangular part with diagonal lines is the tension spring, the circle below it is the idler pulley. Left circle is the variator, right circle is the stock pulley, but you probably figured that out already. You could use any old automobile idler pulley that fits the belt, and maybe you could find a range of springs somewhere that fits your mounting scheme so you could have even more control over variation (just like adjusting a Moby or Pug engine spring, or Hobbit/Vespa rear spring)

i think a gas shock would be your best bet. i'm using a 35 lb. and could go to a 45 no problem. when it variates, it goes through the shock fast. (stock ported with pipe) it only moves about an inch then it's maxed out. so it needs alot of resistance within that span. it takes off and goes pretty good with the 35 lb. but when going up a hill, it needs a touch of launch lever. i think if i had a 45lb, it would be perfect, and hold it back enough to get high rpm's throughout the variation.  i ordered my shock through o'reilly's after i went through their online catalog and picked one that was the right length for where i wanted to put it.

i reinforced the pivot mounts and motor mounts with some 1/4" steel to keep the engine from twisting and wearing out the bushings. i'd say the swinging idea you got there will work, it might need to be more like a parallelogram so the front arm swing is the same as the back arm swing to prevent binding. (mabe not?) and swing from fully up at idle to 1" straight back toward the drive pulley at full variation. i have about 1/2" between the 2 pulleys at full speed. so you might want to start 1/2"-3/4" from the drive and work it forward to determine where the mounts go. ??

when i aligned the belt, i took a small straight edge and lined up the inside side of the drive wheel with the inside (fixed) side of the variator face on the engine. so that when the belt is all the way up it's in line and straight. the engine mount i put on is shifted over about an inch to get it to work.

hope it helps!
