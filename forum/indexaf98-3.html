Post by: Mike Pee on September 22, 2008, 10:48:09 pm
On a Puch, you can do 40 on a stock cylinder with a performance exhaust, a bigger kit, and a dremel.  If you want to get in the 50+ range, you can get just a small kit.  If you want to get in the 60+ range, you can get a big kit.  I take my magnum (big and heavy and slower) on I55 on the way home from work daily now.  I'm hitting low 50s, but it's fine for rush hour highway traffic.

I love working on Puchs, especially ones I haven't touched before.  Bring it on Sunday!  I'll be happy to poke at it.  We'll get it fast.  You should get a 15mm bing, or preferably a small dellorto PHBG if you want to go fast on the stock cylinder.  The proma GP is a good pipe for a stock deal.  Great low end, and better than stock on the mid and high ranges.

My girlfriend's dad got me a homebrewing setup for Chirstmas last year.  We finally got around to using it like 7 weeks ago.  We just sampled our first brew, steam ale, and it's damn good.  You won't regret getting into brewing.

Come on Sunday man!
