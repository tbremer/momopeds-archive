I was thinking about this some more -

On a 2 stroke bike the purpose of the oil is to lubricate the internals - the delivery method is the gasoline.

It stands to reason that during the combustion cycle the majority of the gasoline is vaporized by the heat, leaving the oil, alone, on the internal surfaces where it provides lubrication.

So, on an oil injected engine the oil is introduced after the carburetor. And the gasoline should be at least a mist and at most a vapor at this point.  I suspect then, that the oil is carried into the crankcase by the velocity of the air, and not so much by being dissolved into the gasoline-and-air charge. Is this right?  come on smart guys...

So,  if propane goes in the velocity of the air charge would be similar and would carry the 2 stroke oil to the internal surfaces, hopefully.

It is just a Honda Spree...  so if it seizes it's a small loss and the rest of the bike can be parted out.
