The A jug is always going to be slow, but you can make it LESS slow. Becca said he bike goes 30 or something, so it's really not that bad.

You need some cork gasket b/c your intake is angled. It should bump your stock intake up enough that you can fit the SHA on and then shim it up to the intake. Get a jammy chamber if you want an exhaust for cheap. If you want something nicer you could always get the MLM sachs pipe, but it's not worth it IMO for the A jug. You could also upgrade your header if you want something larger for more flow.

I lucked out with the D jug. My intake is flat so I just flipped it 180 degrees and it faces forward now. The SHA 14.12 mounted perfectly once I pulled the stock shim out, and the jammy clamped right on to the malossi sachs header. It's a ripper...well for a sachs.

So recap:
Cork gasket
jammy pipe
SHA

If you look around you can get some good deals! I think I picked my jammy up, with the clamp for slapping it on the header without welding, for like $30 shipped.

I don't have personal experience doing it, but these guys: //www.mopedarmy.com/forums/read.php?7,2920471,2922209 seem to think it works. Get a micrometer and check the outer diameter of the exhaust just in case. You'll use the micrometer so much; down the road you'll be happy you got it.

They suggested a 15 bing and a biturbo chamber, which is another good option! You could find a bing for cheap too. Don't think my suggestions are the end-all-be-all because you have a few choices, it's up to you. Truth be told, I would go with whatever is cheapest on buy/sell.

The lever choke just means you'll have to pull it by hand on the carb, it's no big deal. You had the shock measuring correct, eyelet to eyelet.

You're right about the SHA, it will be facing the frame but that won't really matter. You'll tune the idle once and never touch it again. The bing would face the right direction if you're concerned.
