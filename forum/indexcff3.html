What would be fun and the right distance away is only accessible by going on highway 40 for a short distance.  That's not allowed since it's also interstate 64.

But anyway, it's to go to the wine country sites just over the highway 40 bridge in Chesterfield.  You go west on highway 94 and there are some neat places to stop and have a glass of wine while overlooking the Missouri River valley.  Highway 94 is a winding road with not TOO many hills to climb.

I also ride out to Castlewood State Park on the Meramac River, and ride the winding roads west of there like Ridge Road, St. Paul Road.  I'll need to hunt for it, but there is a small town at the end of one of those roads that was fun to see.  I think it's by the part of the West Tyson Coungy Park that is north of the Meramac.  The old town of Castlewood (near the park) was an old summertime destination because it had a mineral spring swimming pool we went to y-e-a-r-s ago.  (Think smelly but cool water.) That ride could originate from my house.

I'll try to think of others.
