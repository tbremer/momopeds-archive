This happened to me with one of my legs, it unscrewed while it was in the back of my car. I think (or at least this is how it felt when I screwed it on) there's a slot in the plastic bushing the spring has to sit in just right and then you can get it to rotate and actually have some resistance instead of just spinning around freely. If you can get it in the slot it should seat itself and you can spin it up in there.

It was a complete pain in the ass for me. I read on MA you can damage the plastic bushings pretty easy with the springs so I would be careful.

I figured it out.

1.) Remove the plastic upper-bushing by removing the retaining-pin under the top shim.
2.) Unscrew the spring from the lower-bushings.
3.) Screw the spring carefully back onto the loose upper-bushing.
4.) Re-install the upper-bushing/spring assembly onto the upper-leg.
5.) Open up the lead coil of the spring so that it threads on the lower-bushing more easily.
6.) Screw the lower-leg on.

I found some shit about removing the lower-bushing. I had no luck.
EBRs are cheap shit. Plastic spring anchors? Really?
