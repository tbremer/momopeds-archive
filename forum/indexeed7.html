Around 1979-1980 some guy (who flunked out of engineering school, but managed to get an auto industry job anyway) came up with the idea that it would be real nifty to put a computer into a car.  Computers and autos are inherently incompatable, meaning the former will break or otherwise screw up with some degree of regularity.

Then, those computers, which cost the auto industry less than $20 each, can be sold to the poor car owner for $400 or more (often MUCH more!).  It has proven to be such a moneymaking boondoggle for the auto industry that they heve computerized just about every function on the car, except the driver him/her self.  That's coming up next!

Buy an older vehicle, put in a rebuilt motor, transmission, etc. you will spend much less that the cost of a newer model, and have a more dependable vehicle.
