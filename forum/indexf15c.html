I've got a Garmin Quest 2, and it's got a pretty small screen. On the list of things I don't like about it, the screen size is pretty far down there. Your screen size is only as good as what the GPS is displaying, and if it's in the middle of recalculating a route (which it may or may not do quickly) or re-drawing something (which it may or may not do quickly), or if it's lost satellite reception, then you're just going to have a bigger screen with no useful information displayed on it.

What do you think the biggest advantage of the wider screen is? That it would be easier to see/read? If it's giving you turn-by-turn directions, those are generally more useful than looking at the screen anyway. If you are easily annoyed by the turn-by-turn directions and you'd prefer to look at your planned route on the map, and then figure it out from there (which I've decided is a good plan for mine, since it spends so much time recalculating and not providing any useful information), then the bigger screen might be a plus.

Just as a side note... I might be a little biased on this. For the most part, I really like having a GPS, but I've discovered their limitations in a very frustrating way. It's still definitely an imperfect technology, which only becomes more imperfect if you drop it. FYI - don't drop your GPS.
