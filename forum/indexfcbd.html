Six months later I was able to pick this up again.  I scrapped the initial concept since I couldn't work out the driveline without some expensive parts.   A hobbit setup would allow for easy makings and the fancy engagement lever allows me to use the electric without driveline drag.  So that's what I did.  And I swapped the engine in the frame for a front hub motor. Makes regen braking more effective. 

Got a bit more work done today.  The lower bearing support and internal spacer is done and welded.  It took quite a bit of time on the lathe to get everything right.   The internal spacer also supports the axle in the case of bearing tube deformation or failure.



Bearings and spacing tube all snug on the pivot.




Welded up.  You can see one occlusion (looks like a dimple) where I almost burned through and the dirty interior polluted the molten pool.



Bearing cups welded on.  The bearings were a finger tight press fit before the welding.  Afterwards they needed pressing in with a tool.




Rough mock up of the angles.  With the proper head tube angle it makes the top tube pretty short, so I will either need to extend the wheelbase another 4" or I will have to extend a mount for the gas tank.





Now I just need to ream out the headtube for the headset cups and I can fishmouth the top tube to mate.   Then I can deal with mounting the gas tank.

that's lookin good. the wheel base options depends on what kind of ride you want when your done. if you want it mopedy, keep the wheel base and head tube angle, if you want it long and choppery then extend it out there, right?
it's all about the handling when your taking corners and avoiding speed wobbles

what i did was find a picture of the moped that had the head tube angle that i liked. a real good side shot where the wheels are level and the forks are straight. then i took a piece of paper and put it on my monitor and lined up the bottom edge of the paper with the bottom of the tires in the pic, then i just drew a line and copied the angle of the moped forks on to the paper. then i took a picture of the bike i's working on, loaded it up,and seent if the fork angle line matched up. i had it tack welded so it stayed in place while i adjust it. then i tranfered that shit over to the frame and cut the bar connected to the head tube at that angle. it sounds kinda fuctup, but it's a sure way to get through that part.

check it a million times! clamp a straight edge on the side of the back tire and make the front tire allign.

another thing i did was measure the wheel base on a few bikes. i measured 3'-6" on this other stock bike, then locked the wheel in at that wheelbase and tipped the headtube back to a happy medium angle that was real close to stock.
anyway, glad to see you're back at it.
i build the same way, we don't need no stinkin' frame jigs!  oops!
