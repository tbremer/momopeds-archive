a very easy one ive made before:

Chicken with rice
(originally made in a 13x9 pan)

4-6 chicken thigh/leg quarters (or boneless chicken thighs if preferred)
2-3 cups rice
2-3 cans cream of chicken soup
+water (about 2 cans or so)
seasoning to taste ( i use a cajun seasoning)

mix the uncooked rice with the soup and water in the pan, lightly season the chicken and place evenly spaces in the pan with the rice mixture, cover with foil and bake in oven at 350-375 for about 40 minutes or until done.

VEGGIE version

Substitute  portobello mushroom slices or firm tofu for chicken, substitute cream of mushroom soup. add seasoning to taste.

it goes together easily with only a couple pans to clean.

zucchini- pepper skillet (veggie meal)

1 pound zucchini (washed and cut into 1/4in slices)
1 onion, thinly sliced
1 small green pepper, chopped
2 tablespoons vegetable oil
1 clove garlic, crushed
1 taspoon salt
1/8 teaspoon pepper
2 tomoatoes, cut into wedges

cook/stir zucchini, onion, green pepper, oil, garlic, salt and pepper in a skillet until heated through, cover and cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until veggies are crisp-tender (about 5 minutes) add tomatoes. cover and cook over low heat until tomatoes are heated through.  serve with rice if desired.

4 servings. multiply for larger group

i found a recipe in a cookbook called The Lowmans Guide to Better Starvation

     get a large clean frying pan or grill and turn it on low/ medium
     2 oranges or limes
     1 handful of pine nuts
     1 handful of dried cranberries
     1 large jar of peanut butter
     3 eggs
     1 can of condensed milk
     4 tbsp powdered sugar
     1 bag of bread
     3 mint leafs
     1 bag of self rising flour
     2 tsp vanilla
     1 jar strawberry jelly
     1 head of romaine lettuce
     1 bag of bissinji nuts
     1 box of wonton wraps
     4 tbsp butter or margarine
     

      get the bag of bread out
      pick out a couple slices that are firm and with no holes
      you will need a dull knife for the next step
      open the peanut butter jar and dig out enough to spread across one slice of bread evenly,
      if it seems to be really heavy then take some off
      lick the knife to taste and clean
      next step;
      get out the jar of strawberry jelly and check the expiration date
      open it and dig some of that out, and pretty much follow the first step but on the other slice of
      bread.
      now gently lay the peanut butter bread on top of the jelly bread
      next step;
      turn off the stove or grill
      put the oranges, pine nuts, cranberries, eggs, mint leafs, romaine lettuce, and margarine, back in
      the refridgerator.
      put the condensed milk, flour, vanilla, and wonton wraps back in the cabinet.
      look up bissinji nuts in the dictionary
      all done! that was easy!!!

      just repeat the process if you need to feed more people.
