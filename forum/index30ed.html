Thanks to Brett, Kenden, and Dylan, we started getting my moped taken apart tonight, and so far it doesn't seem to be TOOOO bad. Or it could have been a lot worse, at least.

The forks, front wheel, and front fender are all completely annihilated, bent and broken in every way imaginable. My handlebars and controls are still good, and my grips even survived mostly unscathed (and where they are damaged, I don't care because I'm getting bar-end mirrors after this). The left pedal arm got bent into my flywheel and left a little dent in the flywheel cover, but that doesn't bother me; the dent kinda matches the dent on my knee, and we can bend the pedal arm back out. The left tank plastic is done for, but the right side still looks great. There's a nice dent in the tank on the left side, but with the plastic, it wouldn't be that noticeable ... and again, it kinda matches how my ankle is going to be messed up later on. If I have scars, my bike might as well have scars too. We've come to the tentative conclusion that my frame is bent but that the swingarm is still good, and I know Daryl already offered up his old frame, so I don't have to worry about that part. One of my rear shocks is bent just a little, but I already have another set I had been thinking about putting on but just never got around to it. My cylinder head is missing about 3/4 of a fin, but I think that'll be okay for now. The rest of the engine, carb, and intake all seems like it's good, although the pipe has a rather large dent in it that I think would negatively affect how well it does its job. The rear wheel is still good, as is the seat and rack. I think the rear fender will be okay once we bend it back into shape a little. All in all, I'd say the moped got injured on pretty much the same level I did. It's bad, and it will take time and work to get it as close as back to normal as possible, but it could have been much, much worse.

I'll throw some photos up on Facebook, I think, since that seems easier than linking them in here. Don't worry, I won't post the ankle pic.

I imagine that most of you saw my FB status, but I'll post an update anyway.

I went over to the stupid doctors at 7:30 on Thursday to make my 9am appointment. They took more x-rays of my ankle and said that looked good. Then they said they'd make me an appointment in 4 weeks to re-discuss my my broken pelvis. I talked them down to 3 weeks, and I still wasn't happy about that. Perhaps I should have tried the "impact exercises build strong bones" argument, but I doubt that would have done any good. Then I went over for the surgery part. (All of this included an average of 45 minutes waiting in each area, and none of the waiting rooms are designed for wheelchairs, which is total BS). The doctor told me it was going to be outpatient, but all of his nurses and assistants seemed very perplexed by that order (they don't do communication so well). So I wait around for forever, answer the same questions about 6 times, and finally get taken back there. When I wake up, they're trying to figure out the orders, and we figure out that hey wait, I have no missing skin on my thighs.... WTF. We eventually figure out that they decided to not do the skin graft because it was just too gross and pus-y in there to do it. So they took off the silicon layer that was on the outside of the artificial soft tissue they installed and scrubbed it out really well. I'm doing twice-daily wet-to-dry dressing changes for a week, and then I go back next week when they'll hopefully actually do the skin graft. So basically, I sat around for 12 hours in a hospital and got nothing out of it, except that my foot seems to hurt a little less now (only 1 pain pill today!!)
