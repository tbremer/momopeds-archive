with the pedal arm off the shaft, the pin should slide right in... no hammering neccessary ...the flat side on the pin,should be turned to face the flat side on the shaft,,I believe its what's called an interference fit...the pin's flat side is wedged along its length so the further in it is pulled (with the threads) or tapped, (hammered in) the tighter it gets...Removing a pin usually ruins it. So having spares on hand is recommended....French bikes, have, usually a smaller pins, than the rest...
