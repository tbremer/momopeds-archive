For a mass-produced "Specialty" beer, Fat Tire is pretty decent.

American Lager isn't my cup of tea, let's face it, I'm not 16 anymore. BUT, American Lager makers do one thing better than probably any other beer producer on the planet: Produce Consistency. Fucking flat-out the same beer in every bottle for the past million billion bottles, you'll never taste a difference. So, if you like it, it's a good deal. I've heard this from folks from other countries as well, so I'm not just trying to be patriotic about American beers made for us by us and bought from a foreign company.

I can drink it sometimes, some Mich Ultra if I'm poor or a Bud Select or something, but I absolutely cannot stomach bud light anymore or fucking Nat. light, makes my stomach actually reject the stuff. For cheaps, michelob has some decent efforts at craft beers if you want some flavor and have no monies.

1884 is another nice beer from the New Belgium peoples, nice and black and thick.

But if you're gonna whine about this and that and get all fucking elitist, you can't say with a  straight face that "Fat Tire tastes like dead peoples coin purse" and then be caught fucking dead drinking a miller High Life or PBR. In my opinion, if you're gonna be an elitist about beer, you'd rather go without than dirty your delicate palate with swill. That's just my opinion.

If you like PBR but Fat Tire or Corona or whatever sin't your cup of tea, then that's cool with me.

The best part about beer is that each and every one of us has an opinion on everything we drink.
