For the most part, I do agree. If you know you're going to be riding long distances in a city that's not KC, it's probably not the time to bring your most untrusted POS bike. We should all try to fix as much as we can before we go (and that applies to you, Mr. No-Lights Wiring Mess). However, no matter how much any of us do, someone is almost certainly guaranteed to break down. When that happens, we are going to stop, and wait, and help, and not get all pissy whiny-faced about waiting, because (1) that is what we would want others to do for us if the situation were reversed and because (2) mopedders stick together. We don't know who the moped breakdown monster is going to strike next; it could be you, it could be me, it could be any of us. There's no sense in getting on each other's cases about breakdowns that haven't even happened yet. I think that Missouri hospitality will more than make up for any ghetto shit you people put on your mopeds (and if it doesn't, I don't really care, since I'm from Kansas), and how you treat someone will make much more of an impression on them than how nice or fast or whatever your moped is.
