We just get a MO bill of sale, take it down to the county treasurer's office, tell them we bought it from someone in MO and MO doesn't register motorized bicycles so no I don't have a title and no I don't need a title, give them money, sign 10 papers, and walk out with a plate. Then they mail you a title a few weeks later. Some of the guys have had to get the mopeds inspected (it all depends on who you talk to and what mood they're in that day), but I never have. It would only be a problem if the moped had been registered in KS before, I think, and I don't think the chances are too high of that happening. The less money you put as the "bought" price, the less you have to pay in taxes (I usually put down $50, so it's about as low as you can go and still be believed), although there's always the $20 registration fee and a few other random fees they tack on.

And my spring project: make all 4 of my mopeds work at the same time. 4 for 4, 100% working mopeds. That's all I want.
