Title: Re: Tomos A3 Runs only when partially choked
Post by: OldF4rt on July 06, 2008, 08:32:38 am
Brian,

When you check plug color, look at the porcelian below the contacts.  Don't worry about the metal around the outer edge or the contacts themselves.  The porcelain gets hottest when the engine runs and that's when the color is developed. 

You might have heard of different "heat range" of plugs.  A hotter plug just has a longer porcelain.  It goes up higher into the metal around the outside of the plug (inside the threads) before it is bonded to the metal.  That means it cannot dump its heat as easily as one that contacts the outside metal just barely inside the threaded metal.  So it's the porcelain that develops the most heat and can burn to the proper color. 

I see your thoughts on the oil in the gasoline causing a darker tint to the porcelain color.  The article is talking about motorcycles.  Some do and some don't burn oil with the gasoline, but I think the article is talking about big bikes that don't burn oil with the gas so I assume they would have a lighter color.  The oil does cause oily deposits on the metal at the end of the threads for sure and could be darkening the porcelain as well.

Dave
