I'm feeling a little better every day. My legs still hurt, especially my knees. My knees are where the most muscular trauma occurred during the accident, and where they inserted the rods. However, I can bend them a little more each day. I'm still pretty much bed-ridden, but I've been practicing walking/ my walker. I can make it about 50 feet or so before it gets too difficult. The hardest part about walking w/ the walker is getting up and sitting down, tho I can now do both w/o assistance. I really can't wait till I am on crutches, but that can't happen until I get the cast off my hand. It's ironic that crutches would mean freedom, but to me they do. I was staying at my mother's house in Ballwin, but am now staying w/ my friend's parents in Kirkwood, as they are recently retired and have time to help me out. I also returned to school last week. It was rough but it was good. I know I've got a good excuse for dropping this semester, but this was supposed to be my last year and I don't want to wait any longer!
