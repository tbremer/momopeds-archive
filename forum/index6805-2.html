Uhhhhh!








This is the start to my next real build.   I'm kinda petered out on working on the Tomos a3, so I need something to occupy my mind.  This bike will combine the variated Ciao with an electric motor capable of 40hp peak (you can see the frame of the motor tucked in there).  I will be limiting the power to under 10hp for control, drivetrain, and legality reasons. 

Designed around 16" wheels, and 7" of travel front and back.  This will have pedals.   


Accumulated parts list

Vespa Ciao engine with front variator
3230 Shumaker/Astro, 50kv Wye
Marzocchi Super T Fork
Chris king headset
Hayes disc brake kits
1000w/h of lipo batteries
Spokes
Front hub


There are many details to sort out still, mostly drivetrain and rear suspension clearances. The rear suspension will have a low pivot with jackshaft to connect the three powers. Pedaling will be reserved for low speed help and maneuvering, as well as emergency use.

Needs list:
Suitable motor controller
Rear variator/ clutch for engine
Jackshaft
bearings
lots of chromo tubes and plate
gas tank
rear hub



I will hopefully start on the frame jig in a few days.  First item is to clean out my garage.  It is time to thin stuff out.

The jackshaft is for reduction, attachment of the "rear" variator, and to bring pedal, electric, and gas power all together easily.  From the jackshaft there will be either #40 or #35 chain to a rear sprocket.  I'm either going to use a light motorcycle hub or a bicycle hub.  I'm kinda leaning towards a custom hub with double ISO disc mount so I don't have to worry about weak ass freewheels on the rear.
