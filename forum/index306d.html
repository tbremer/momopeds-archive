LOL at hydroforming 30 gauge stainless plate - that would be what's called a pipe bomb.

I'm sure someone can do it, but I have some of the same stuff I've pulled off of commercial refrigerators. I use it to make sidecovers and brackets and the like, and the stuff is effing tough. Tough and just a tad bit brittle.

Cutting it is a pain. You quickly learn to design forms that take the fewest cuts, or that use long, straight cuts that you can cut halfway and then break (score and snap).

The finished products are indestructible, though.
