Not nearly as advanced as what you guys have going, but a personal triumph, nonetheless. Not knowing much, I bought this QT50 from Gruff last year, rode it most of the summer, and then broke it. I've been working on it all winter, and have added a kit, carb, air filter, tighter clutch springs, and a Jemco pipe. I've eliminated the oil injection system, replaced the throttle cable to get rid of the ugly junction box, replaced a couple of elements of the drive train that were worn, gave it all new gaskets, new crank seals and bearings, redone the fuel lines, and redone the headlight to eliminate the sealed beam lamp. I also cleaned off a bunch of the gunk off and am painting the trim pieces, just for yucks.

Got it up to forty today, and for a first try, I'm pretty proud of it. It's been fun learning on it, and the fact that I brought it back from the dead is pretty gratifying.
