When you have sealed bearings you don't need a nut on each side.

As far as 6001s (12x28x8) vs 6201s (12x32x10)... I'm not sure about snowflakes, but you can't get 6201s on Puch spokes without making the openings in the hub larger.  I'd rather keep material there and have a slightly lower load capacity of 1100lbs vs 1500lbs per bearing.

It's not the loading on the bearings that's the issue. It's heat, how many RPMs can they take before they no longer can shed heat fast enough. They only have to get hot once to fuck with the steel's temper.
You should always go with the heaviest duty bearing you can fit, IMO.
Removing hub material is bad too. Trade-offs...
