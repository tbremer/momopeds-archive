I like this.

The only problems I see are:

-without a shroud, only the right side of the cylinder will be getting fan cooled (uneven cooling, maybe an issue?), and it'll only work when you're stopped. When you're moving, the forced air would probably disrupt the fan air. This might or might not cause problems, what do you think?
-WITH a shroud, the whole cylinder would be fan-cooled (same config as every air cooled scooter ever), but all forced air from moving would be gone, so you'd be relying solely on the fan; and if you don't give it ENOUGH air, obviously you'll run into problems. I've read about this happening a lot with H-Minarelli scooters - they kit and the stock fan isn't able to keep it cool enough.

But it looks really solid and I'd like to see this materialize.

Looking back at the pictures I posted it is missing a section of the fan housing that goes across the bottom of the cylinder.   My plan it to box in the cylinder and leave the head exposed, simular to the v1 design.  That way the cylinder that isn't geting air anyway when it is variated can get some flow and the head can get its normal cooling by convential air flow.

Justin has a valid point about uneven cooling thought.  Unlike the Minarelli cylinders the Gila kit has a much thicker cylinder wall and variable fin thickness.  That might mean that air from the fan might not flow around the cylinder effectively and only one side will be cooled. 

But no point in not trying it out.  I am going so a aluminum fiberglass composite, making the parts that touch the cylinder out of aluminum and the rest out of fiberglass.  I should have some progress photos by tomorrow.  But I would still like to get your opinions.

Last night I went to ACE and looked around for things that I could hack up to make this fan shroud.  I found a large diameter snap pip fitting, some aluminum sheet, fiberglass risen, cloth, and randome hardware. I took it all home and made the basic form that I will coat with fiberglass tonight

The fan housing is made out of the snap fitting, which is cut on the sides to inset two long nuts which will be bonded to the fitting with some glass work.  The parts that touch the cylinder are made out of the sheet aluminum and will get fiberglass laid on to parts of it and then rivits run through once its hardened for extra support.
