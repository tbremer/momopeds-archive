Kevin's been having rides and fixits? Don't feel bad; he didn't invite me either.

If Kevin is fixing his mopeds by himself, I guess that makes four of us who have been working on our mopeds alone this week.

So, it appears that there is a problem with the fix it day concept (among other things). If we ride on Sundays, people show up with broken mopeds, and we spend too much time fixing the broken mopeds and not enough time riding. If we plan fix-it days, people don't show up or can't come. I think Kevin has a point about people not wanting to pack things up to come -- either him or anyone else. As far as I know, I'm the only one who is really set up for mobile repairs. Yes, I'm sure we could all use the tools at whoever's house we're at, but that would be annoying if we all needed the same tools at the same time, and that doesn't solve the inconvenience of having to transport project mopeds back and forth. Kevin, I'd be more than happy to pick up you and your moped and transport it to Chuck and Matt's house for a fix-it day, but I obviously can't do that if I can't come to fix-it. (That offer applies to anyone and everyone in KC or between KC and Lawrence).

Kevin, I think that your "name the fix-it" days sound good in theory, but it wouldn't work with the reality of mopeds. Not only will everyone always have 10 different things to fix, even if you got a few people who all had similarly-themed repairs, there's no saying that something else with a higher fixing priority wouldn't break before the designated fix-it day. I'm not saying it wouldn't work occasionally and for big projects, but I wouldn't want to rely on that plan to fix the day-to-day stuff that goes wrong with mopeds.

Since at least four of us have proven just in this discussion that we are perfectly capable of working on our mopeds alone (well, at least the rest of you have proven you're capable -- me I'm not so sure about...), how badly do we need a group fix-it anyway? Is it just to help the newbs? How many newbs do we have? And could/would they even show up at a regular fix-it day anyway? I don't know that this idea would work any better than Kevin's, but maybe fix-it days should be "by appointment only." As in, if someone has something that is broken, and it is beyond their expertise, capabilities, or tool set to fix, then they can put out a "Help me!" message. Then, anyone with the expertise (etc) to help could set up a time to help the person fix whatever is wrong. If anyone besides the help-ee or helper wanted to join in, then they could, but nobody besides the two original people would be EXPECTED to be there. I'm just thinking that this would let us ignore the stress of group fix-its while still letting people who need help get help, and would thus allow us to focus on riding (which is the priority for most of us anyway, yes?)

Also, as a general observation about fixing stuff... when you're a newb, I honestly think you end up being more knowledgeable if you DON'T automatically have people around to help you. I knew NOTHING about mopeds or anything mechanical when I first got a moped. Naturally, it broke. I didn't have anyone to fix it for me or help me fix it (other than through the power of the interwebz), so I had no choice but to get in there and do it myself.  Yes, there is a difference between helping someone do something and doing it for them, but how often does "help me do this" quickly become "do this for me"? Matt, when people know that there's someone as knowledgeable as you around, it's soooooo temping to say, "Well, I could do this, but it's too hard/I don't know enough/I might screw it up, so I should let Matt help me/do it for me." And when that happens, people don't learn as much as they could. I don't know, maybe I've got this backwards -- maybe you'd rather help people out before they fuck up stuff with a gung-ho DIY attitude (and then REALLY need help fixing it), but from my experience, you learn a lot more when you do things on your own.

Bottom line: I sense unhappiness here. I don't like unhappiness. Mopeds make me happy. Riding mopeds makes me happy. You <i>all</i> make me happy. Riding mopeds with you <i>all</i> makes me REALLY happy. I want you <i>all</i> to be happy. How can I make you all happy? If I can't make you happy, can you at least lie to me and tell me that there is something I can do to make you happy, and then let me do it, and then lie to me again and tell me you're happy? Please? Happy?
