Sam in your example, the inside cheek is connected to a shaft which goes inside the outside cheek.  The outside cheek has a larger cylinder that houses the spring and would spin freely, except the larger cylinder on the outside cheek is bolted to the inner shaft of the inside cheek.  Thats how most scooter contra's / clutches work.  It can't work like that if there is a pedal shaft sticking through where the bolt would be..

Doing some drawings and trying to figure it out, my only solution would be to have the inner shaft of the inside cheek notched so that the outter cheek has grooves to "ride in" that will link the two together.
