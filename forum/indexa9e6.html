Title: Re: If only all cars were 2-stroke....
Post by: EuGene on March 01, 2010, 09:51:42 am
A-ko, it has been so many years since I have seen either type of car that I could easily confuse the twain, especially if viewed from the front

However, there are a few easy ways to distinguish a Bianchina from a Vespa from the front:

The Bianchina headlights, and the upper fender behind them project slightly beyond the lower wheel well area of the fender.  The Vespa headlights are closer together and located slightly inside the fender sides.

The Bianchina headlights project forward from the fenders with chrome trim rings.  The Vespa headlights are slightly Frenched (inset) and do not have chrome rings.

The Bianchina has white rubber bumper overriders located at the ends of the bumpers and directly under the headlights with the turn signals inside the overriders.  The vespa has chrome overriders located below the inside edge of the headlights with the bumper extending beyond the overriders and the turn signals located directly under the headlights and outside the overrider locations.

I would love to have one of each!!  I used to have 3 Bianchinas and 1 Vespa & loved 'em all.
