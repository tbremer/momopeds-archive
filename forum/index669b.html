I'm not real sure there are a couple of things that would be really helpful namely the puch factory tools (Please Dude Send Me A Freaking Picture Of That Damn Box!) Due to the minute tolerances that are needed to build a blueprinted (Build to exact factory specifications) engine. That being said, the good thing is the engine doesn't have to be blueprinted to be solid. Parts are expensive and less available than those for the E-50. lastly IMHO the tuning is more equivalent to that of a 12-string guitar where the E-50 is your classical 6-string.

Points of interest;
ZA-50s have a bushing top crank, don't fuck it up taking the wrist pin out of the piston, new stock replacement cranks are twice that of performace E-50 cranks.

Thare be a lot-o-shims involved in the clutch alignment. Couple of ziplock baggies, two  numbered wooden dowel rods about 6" long , first gear clutch shit goes on #1 in the order you pull it off, second gear same as the first. Take your time here, be careful not to lose the shims on the front of each gear shaft, mine had one on each, they sometimes stick to the bearings int the front of the cover. Take digi pics if you can, they can save your ass later.

I can't stress it enough, while disassembling the engine, take your time. Protect cover plate gasket with your life, even if you cut / have a new one! You need that original gasket thickness to know if you need to increase your shim thickness to get within spec (remember those shims and the cover plate). On that not measure every gap you can, lip to seal, lip to bearing with seal removed, etc.. having these measurements will help you get back to where you are shimmed now.

There is a ZA-Rebuild and trouble shooting thread somewhere in repair and performace area here and search for all of Paz's posts on MA for the teardown how-to's with links to his YouTube videos, think there is also a thread where I posted the internet store where i got my bearings, The seals and shims are on treats.

I'll see if I can dig up those links.
