If you don't want to spend any money you can:
-bore out the stock 12mm bing carburetor to 14mm (I've never done this, but I've read it's not hard on Moped Army)
-drill some holes in the exhaust baffles - just like 2...maybe 3.  But start with 1, see how that effects things, try another, etc.
-port the intake and exhaust
-take apart the wheel bearings, clean, and regrease them
-make sure your chain is well lubed
-make sure your timing is set right

If you want to spend a little bit of money:
-buy a performance exhaust
-buy a 15m bing carb (and a bunch of jets)
-if it's a one speed, you can tune the clutch to engage later, or get a three shoe clutch and tune that
-port the intake and exhaust
-take apart the wheel bearings, and replace them
-replace the chain
-make sure your timing is set right

If you want to spend a lot of money:
-buy a kit (anywhere from 65cc to 80cc - in general: the bigger the faster, but also harder to tune right and usually more expensive)
-match the transfer ports in the kit cylinder to the transfer ports in the case
-buy a performance exhaust
-buy a big dellorto carb (19mm or 21mm) and a bunch of jets
-make sure the intake and exhaust ports match the new intake and exhaust you're potting on there - if not, port them to match
-take apart the wheel bearings, and replace them
-replace the fork, or at least rebuild the stock one
-replace the chain
-make sure your timing is set right
-get new tires and tubes

If you want to spend your life savings and try to break 70mph:
-buy an 80cc kit (like the 80cc gilardoni reed valve kit)
-bore out your engine case to accept the huge sleeve
-match the transfer ports in the kit cylinder to the transfer ports in the case (you can send the case to 1977 mopeds to have this done for a good chunk of change)
-buy a jollymoto brand or other handmade exhaust
-buy a huge carb (like a 24mm mikuni flat slide)
-make a custom intake for the carb
-buy a fully stuffed racing crank, new bearings, and new oil seals to rebuild a 1 speed engine
-buy a three shoe clutch and tune it
-buy a CDI ignition system
-buy new wheels, or NOS ones
-buy a new chain
-buy new tires and tubes
-buy a new fork
-fabricate a disc brake system - you'll need it

Pick your poison.  If you do the last one, get a helmet and life insurance.
