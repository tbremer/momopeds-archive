Been running the 3-shoe as of late.  Lighten the shoes and rip.  Just make sure they weigh the same and don't cut off the rests.  I got my clutch to slip right into my pipe.  Still gotta fine tune them.  Motomatic has pics on how they lighten their 3 shoes, check it out.

So I would say 2 shoe for easiness of tuning and 3 shoe for performance.

The key to e-50 performance is the clutch, which is often mistaken for shitty jetting.

I haven't found any smidge of difference in performance running a 2 shoe or a 3 shoe.  The key is to balance the arms so they engage at the same time.  Other than that it's just tightening springs.  Then again I like a semi-early engagement for longevity purposes...I'm sick of spending $ on puch clutches.  haha
