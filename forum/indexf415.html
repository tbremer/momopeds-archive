Not too much carb.  but if your intake isn't 15mm, and your intake port isn't 15mm, you're wasting the effort.  I'd start two jets above your stock 12mm jet, and do a plug chop.  If it looks close, adjust the needle clip up or down accordingly.  Moving the clip up on the needle would lean it out, and down would richen it.  You should be rich, and have to lean it out either by downjetting or by changing the needle.

What cylinder do you have this on?

On a bing moving the needle is pretty easy.  Just slides out of the grove on the needle, and goes back in any of the 4 grooves you want to put it in.  Like I said, moving the clip up on the needle would lean it out, and down would richen it.  But that is a fine tuning adjustment.  You'll want the needle in the middle to start, while you figure out which main jet you'll need.  Then fine tune the needle once you've got the main jet figured out.  Or at least that's how I do it.

Do you have a 15mm intake for it too?  If not, Treats has them.  And once you get that you'll want to dremel the outside intake port to match the intake.

And you'll want to dremel the outside exhaust port to match the internal diameter of the proma gp.

Cylinder off, dremel ports to size, use air compressor to blow out metal shavings, coat inside with 2 stroke, reassemble (being careful not to bust rings), then ride it like you stole it.
