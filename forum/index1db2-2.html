 this is how the pipe comes into the rectangle base plate. i cut the base plate first, using the rectangle hole size on the inside that the reed valve fits into and bolted it in place, the bolt pattern was from the original dio intake,, then held the intake pipe up to it at the angle i needed, eyeballed a line at the intersection of the 2 parts, hacked off the angle, held it up there again, tack welded the tip of the intake pipe in the center of the base plate hole, held the carb up about where it's gonna be on the end of the intake pipe, bent the pipe around (the tip is tack welded)til the carb didn't hit anything, tack the other side of the pipe to the base.  i'll have to take more pics to explain the rest
Title: Re: who likes puzzles?
Post by: R.I.P. on January 27, 2011, 09:44:11 pm
this is them homemade dio intake parts.
i cut these pieces out of a 1/4" chunk of steel. on the 1st piece i cut the outside shape then put a hobbit intake over it and clamped it, then drilled through both to get the bolt hole lay out right. then cut the hole out of the middle to match the hole in the cases. i used a jig saw with a metal bit.
the 2nd piece is the same deal matching the reed valve hole size. clamp and drill.
so, that's the top and bottom. then i bolted the 1st piece to the engine and held the 2nd piece over it facing back, looking to see if i could get the  mounting bolts in and out easy. it measured 1 1/8" on the back at that angle, so i cut and tacked that together. then it could be adjusted to find the front length. i cut that one 3/8" and tacked it. the sides are just wedges with the point cut off and the back trimmed.
the side material is 10 gauge steel. after i put it together i welded some nuts to the back side of the 2nd piece to make it easier to install and/or take apart.
Title: Re: who likes puzzles?
Post by: R.I.P. on January 27, 2011, 10:03:47 pm
then i welded that all good. i put some long bolts in the bottom 2 bolt holes on the case then cut off the heads with the threads sticking up 5/8" in order to make studs for the bottom of the intake to fasten.

i cut out another 2nd piece to make a base for the carb to attach to it all. it sets on top of the reed valve when it's all done.
the area that is cover in black marker is where i sliced into the pipe to take the circle shape and start transitioning it to the rectangle shape. so looking at it, that is the shape of the pieces that i had to make fit. when you're looking at the outside (as in the pic) the cut on the tube is in a "V" shape. that's how to knock the sides of the circular tube out and get it to start spreading the flow out to the rectangle shape. it looks a little crooked and uneven because i had to twist it and make it work in this situation to clear the frame and the peddle sprocket. it helped to put the carb on when adjusting everything because it has to fit when it's all put together.
