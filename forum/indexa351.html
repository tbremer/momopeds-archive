Are you having problems getting it started, or is it running like crap? The lack of air filter might have something to do with that. Do you choke it when you try to start it? That should cut down on some of the extraneous air flow and make it easier to start (if too much air is the problem, anyway). If it's running like crap, put some cloth over the air intake part of the carb, or use tape to cover up half(ish) of that hole. If it runs better, then you should upjet or get an air filter.

You've got just the stock Bing on it, right?



That little rod is attached to the choke plate inside the carb. When you push it down, it pushes the choke plate into the path of air movement. If you look down the hole in the back of your carb (where your air filter would usually be) you should be able to see the plate move up and down. When you twist the throttle, the choke plate will move up, un-choking the moped. If it starts choked and doesn't run well, you could try partially taping up or covering the hole I circled. Covering the hole somewhat might also help it start, but the choke should already be blocking most of that hole, so maybe not.
