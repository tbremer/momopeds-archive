Re: Grande speedo drive & cable
« Reply #5 on: June 07, 2010, 06:49:02 pm »
When you hold it in your right hand with the tab (that turns with the wheel) sticking out towards the left and turn it in the same manner the wheel would turn it, look down in the hole where the cable plugs in and see which direction the square cable socket turns.  If it is turning clockwise, then it is the correct drive thingy.

If you can't see down in the dark hole to tell which way it is turning then try this:

Hold it in front of you with the part where the cable is inserted pointing upwards and the side that would face the brake drum pointed away from you, then

if it looks like this -----> d  it will turn counter-clockwise and is the wrong one

if it looks like this -----> b  it will turn clockwise and is the correct one!!

Yes, it would be weird for the cable to turn the wrong way.  The the driver I have is made to be mounted on the left side of the front wheel, so if it is put on the right side it will run backwards.  The normal place for the Grande speedo drive is on the right side so I need a driver made for that side.  I think the one I have was off a Ciao, at least some models (if not all) which had the speedo drive on the left side.

I don't know if turning the front wheel around would work, since the forks might interfere with the brake lever (it being in the trailing position rather than forward), the brake shoes operating backwards, etc.  Even though all sorts of "different" things are regularly done to modify our mopeds, methinks just putting the correct speedo drive on it would be the simplest solution.  That is one shaped like a "b" rather than a "d".
