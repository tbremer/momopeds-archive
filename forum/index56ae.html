alright. So now I have 3 designs for the drive train....

#1: link motors lik motors directly together by extending the cranks and welding them together. This leaves me with out the possibility of sticking my transmission on it, which would be cheaper, but not as fun, or fast
#2: link the two motors directly to the new transmission, linking the two cranks by the driving gear. This would maximize power to the rear wheel, and provide less wear on the cranks
#3: link motors together using a centrifugal clutch that would engage both the engines at different times. The clutch will be connected to a differential that would lead a drive shaft into the transmission. This would decrease wear on the transmission and cranks, but would also decrease the amount of force excreted onto the back wheel. On the other hand, it will let me variate

I don't know which one to choose. they all have their ups and downs
