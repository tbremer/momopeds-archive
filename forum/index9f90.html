Repair and Performance / Carburetor Tuning and plug chops
« on: July 05, 2008, 07:58:30 pm »
I found an article about motorcycle carburetor tuning and I would like us to put our heads together and see if we can apply the information to simpler moped carburetors.  I will first give you a link of where the article is, so you can read it yourself.  Then, I want to summarize key points that I'm hoping we can apply to moped carburetors that do not have all the adjustments a motorcycle carburetor has.  The article is at:
https://www.johnsmotorcycleparts.com/Keihin_CV_tuning_tips.htm

What interests me from the article is the idea we should do multiple plug chops to see where our carburetor problem is.  AND we should always use NEW sparkplugs.  The article says motorcycle carburetors have 1.) idle circuits 2.) midrange circuits and 3.) high speed circuits.  I know most of our moped carburetors have the midrange and high circuits, but I have not figured out the idle circuits form mine yet.

The midrange circuit is the needle attached to the throttle slide.  The size, shape and position (up and down) of the needle gives us proper medium speed cruising.  The main jet controls fuel flow at wide open throttle (WOT).

The article tells us to do a plug chop by putting in a new plug, run the motorcycle at the range (right now, I'll say medium speed or WOT) and kill the engine and inspect the plug.  The article explains that we will no longer see the tan color we all see in the books about mopeds.  Leaded gasoline gave that tan color, and we don't have lead in the gasoline any more.  NOW WE NEED EGGSHELL WHITE!  We look at the ceramic surface below the spark gap and nothing else.  So, for example, we would put in a new plug, run the moped for a mile or two at mid-throttle, kill the engine and inspect the plug.  Then, we would find an open area, PUT IN A NEW PLUG and run the moped at WOT for a mile or two.  Again, we would kill the engine and do an inspection of the plug color.

Then, we need to figure out what our carburetor "idle circuit" is and do a third plug chop (with another new plug) with the engine idling.  Actually, the article implies this is the first plug chop, but you get the idea.

Interesting stuff!  Can we apply it to mopeds?

General Discussion / Chinese mopeds
« on: June 30, 2008, 08:18:43 am »
A front page article in today's (June 30th) Post Dispatch reports that "Dirty engines from China (are) a problem in U.S."

It seems anyone can order a shipping container full of gasoline powered equipment, from generators to mopeds and those engines are polluting the air more than engines produced in the U.S. with government specifications on exhaust emissions.  How much more?  The article quotes one sale of 200,000 chain saws that will pump out 268 tons of carbon emissions over the lifetime of those chainsaws.

In the future, the EPA might try to track those chainsaws (and generators and Chinese mopeds) to their current owners to get them removed from service and help reduce the smog problems in some cities.  Can anyone say "PopCycle?"

Next they might try attacking mopeds created before smog emissions were of interest.  Lawn mowers are already of interest.  An Internet search for "emissions from lawn mowers" found the sentence "One old gas powered lawn mower running for an hour emits as much pollution as driving 650 miles in a 1992 model automobile."  Today's article reports lawn mowers and other small engine equipment might soon be required to have catalytic converters to reduce emissions.  That won't make them cheaper to buy.

Well, I need to stop writing this.  I gotta' go fire up my weed eater and my lawn mower and cut and trim the grass.  Oh, and I have been meaning to do a test run of my backup generator.  Put on your gas masks!!!!
