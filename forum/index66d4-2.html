C'mon Mike, you can't pretend like you've never wanted to make this kind of thing happen. It would be a lot of fun and it would solve the rally transportation problem, if only we could make the logistics work.

Unfortunately, I don't think any of us would be able to make the logistics work. If we wanted to rent it out to frats and sororities, we'd need a driver, we'd need to advertise, we'd need people to field questions and plan out whatever business we got, plus we'd need even more insurance, which would probably be hideously expensive. I'm kind of doubting that anyone would volunteer for setting up the insurance just for our personal use, much less if we wanted to set it up so we could cart stupid drunk college kids around on weekends.

Anything we did to it, someone would be getting screwed over. Honestly, probably whoever was doing most of the work would be the one getting screwed over the most. Whoever called to set up insurance would have to pay, and then what if they didn't get paid back? What if half of us are dead broke when it needs fixing, so someone buys parts and never gets paid back? Who's going to keep an accounting of all of the expenses, and even then, there will be disagreements about what a "necessary" expense is -- what if someone refuses to chip in on a repair they don't agree with? Demanding money up front would help, but that never works when we're just paying for gas for rallies, so I'm dubious that it would work when we're talking about much more complicated and expensive things.

Basically, I think that someone with sufficient dedication and time could overcome the logistical issues of all of this, but I don't think they could overcome the financial obstacles. And even if they could, who wants that job? Not I, said the Lindar.
