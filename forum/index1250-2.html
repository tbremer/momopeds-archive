On my drive home, they were talking about engine stalls in light airplanes and how pilots instinctively want to pull up to regain control.  However, on light planes this kills the engine, the pilot, and the passengers pretty quickly. 

What you are supposed to do is put the nose down (which is counter-intuitive) but it allows the plane to get lift under the wings.  This allows the pilot a chance to regain control. 

The discussion was in the context of the Air France disaster last year.   Information on that disaster is still fragmentary, but it seems the pilots were fighting stalled engines for 3+ minutes before the plane was ripped apart.  Data seems to suggest that they kept trying to pull up to restore control but in fact it only made things worse...

They did draw a sharp distinction between an air-liner and a light plane in terms of the success of this maneuver...
