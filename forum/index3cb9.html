I just got my cases together, but I was a real bitch. I can't even turn the crank by hand, but it does turn with a little pressure using channel locks, and I put a little two stroke in and that helped. I've read all kinds of stuff to the effect that it's pretty normal until you get it started and burn the thick grease out of the bearings. I hope. I got pretty stuck on the seals and which way they are supposed to go on a Moby. Tons of conflicting advice on the matter, but I finally got it straightened out, which for the record, is with the springs facing out, or AWAY from the bearings, or flat side out. After I find some circlips for the piston, I'm calling the engine done temporarily, until it runs and I know I'm not gonna screw it up for my new airsal kit and the ninja. Pretty excited for that, whenever that may be. I want my copper moby frame back now!
