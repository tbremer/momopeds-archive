Get with Jimmy and borrow his Honda clutch puller. A 35mm socket is handy in removing the variator nut. A huge flat head, a rubber mallet for banging the crank out and a giant dose of patience. Have lots of zip ties ready when you do crack the engine so you can keep all the cogs and washers in a somewhat organized fashion. Also, there is one part that has these two bearings and a spring lodged in it. There is a good chance you'll take that off and they'll pa twang everywhich a way. Good luck. You'll figure it out I'm sure.
