The law will be overturned upon judicial challenge.  You can't ban vehicles from the road that have been DOT approved for road use.  That's just discrimination.

Most places that passed the "nothing under 50cc" laws did it in response to those pocket bikes that were popular a few years ago.  People rode those around, and the police had no ordinance (that they knew of) that they could ticket them for.  Well, they could have impounded their pocket bikes, or given tickets, because they were riding non-DOT approved vehicles.  Unfortunately local police, and local politicians aren't very intelligent.  They passed these stupidly restrictive laws to get rid of them, and effectively got rid of legitimate, DOT-approved ways for licensed drivers to get around.

If they keep this law in effect, we should swarm Chesterfield.  Yes, PopCycles were sold there, and I've personally driven a number of 49cc and 50cc vehicles on Chesterfield roads with no problems, nor any feeling of being unsafe doing so.

Interestingly, our mopeds are a true 50cc (well, some of them - I'm 99% sure Puchs are).  So the state won't license 50cc or under, and Chesterfield prohibits anything under 50cc.  But we're just at that cutoff where we're exempt by state law, and Chesterfield can't touch us.  To be sure of this I'd need to see an actual copy of the Chesterfield ordinance, but that is my impression of it from the article.
