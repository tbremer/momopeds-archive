My enjoyment of mopeds began about 50 years ago when I was 13.  I scrounged up a beat up looking Jawa 175 motorcycle that ran like the dickens, but had a lot of electrical trouble, bad brakes, worn out tires, etc. and I didn't have the money to fix it.  So I swapped it for a Allstate Puch moped which I loved dearly.  Rode the Hell out of it for about 2 years, including many eighty mile round trips back to my hometown.

Loved that ped and quite unlike the Jawa, it never once broke down, though I did get stranded a few times by running out of gas.  Since then I have owned another Allstate Puch and a couple of regular Puch mopeds and a couple of Peugeots.  I now have another Peugeot (& a parts bike) which I have been rebuilding - on hold now 'til warmer weather gets here.

I also have a couple of Solex motorized bikes.  My first Solex was at about age 16, later my second one was as a early 20's soldier in Saigon, Vietnam, so I was ready to find another one.  With help and encouragement from IRE Mark, I soon scarfed up the two that I currently ride.

I was in to restoring old cruiser bicycles first, and I used to toll craigslist relentlessly trying to find old bicycles to restore.  One day there was a misscatagoried post for two moped for 120$ that I could not pass up.  I called the guy and he told me if I could be to his house in a half hour he would sell them to me because I was in town and he was tired of people constantly calling him.  I got there and found a black Batavus Regency toptank and a orange Honda Express.  I hated the Express and eventually sold the thing for 200$... awesome.

According to him his daughter had won the Batavus in a rafle back in the 70's and within a month of riding it she curbed it.  When I bought it the front wheel was a tocko the fork was bent and the engine was seized from sitting for 30 years.  I have to admit I knew nothing about working on mopeds at that point and it was really hard to find parts for a Batavus.  I used a fire wrench to break the seize, got a new piston an rings and rebuilt the front end, but thanks to an electrical problem it would shock you if you touched anything metal, including the gas tank, which was bad.  I had no idea what to do, but thanks to a post for help on moped army I found out about Chuck and Matt in Kansas City and we got the problem under control in time to ride in the Kansas City rally two years ago. 

Even though I broke down and lost track of everyone I was hooked.  Since then I have stuck with the Batavussss... I picked up a Starflight off of ebay for 70$ and restored it, and I bought another Regency toptank that was in a shed for 60$ from a guy who ran me off the road when I was riding one day to tell me he had a moped just like mine. 

It wasn't until this past summer that I really became apart of the moped community thanks to a random message from Mike B. who found my info on moped army.  Now I am completely obsed an only wish I had more money to wast on mopeds.
