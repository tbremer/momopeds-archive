Use high octane fuel for high compression engines. This is massively generalized, but higher compression generates extra heat. If the heat gets too high, the fuel will ignite before the spark plug sparks (this is pre-ignition, or pinging). Higher octane fuels are more resistant to being ignited by heat alone, so you have a better chance of your spark plug igniting the fuel at the proper time in the cycle.

(I apologize to anyone who understands that better than I do for butchering proper science/technology.)
