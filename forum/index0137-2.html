INFO: Puch Polini
« on: September 01, 2011, 07:39:47 pm »
Well i'm not sure if anyone in town has messed with the Puch 64cc Polini cylinder for Puch (if you have then please chime in!) so i'd like to share my experiences.

When you get the cylinder you must gap the ring --- it is NECESSARY or it will get caught on the exhaust port and bye bye new kit/new piston or BOTH.  Don't spend $225 and forget to chamfer the ports too.  The ports come pretty darn smooth but you really wanna get in there and chamfer them as well as you can.  I kinda got lazy with it but they are SMOOTH and acceptable to my standards (which might be a little low if you know me well enough).

You've basically got two reed petal/block options --- the one that comes with the polini has an intake for a 15mm Bing.  The other option is to buy the Malossi 4 petal reed block with carbon reeds that is made for Peugeot.  It will fit the intake but you have to get a few shorter bolts and cut off the 4 or 5 small fins between the two large fins on top of the head.  You must also sand the intake surface to a smooth finish.  It is not smooth from the factory so you must smooth it down or your cylinder will get a nice airleak.  If you use the Malossi block you also must find some way of sealing the "lip" that sits on the front of the cylinder... you'll see it if you look.  That is the first place it will spring a leak for some reason.  I usually goop mine up with a ton of red RTV and let it sit for 24hrs to cure... it won't look pretty but you also won't really see that part unless you're looking hard.

Make sure you case match.  If you're gonna go pro you might as well case match because there are two vents on the cylinder that will help cool the intake side of the piston down... if you don't cut them into the case you might run a little too hot and seize on the intake side.  Just port it.  Sam does a good job (hint hint).  Plus, the transfers are huge.  Just case match, your cylinder will be happy and so will you.

Polini's LOVE FUEL.  I have a 24mm flatslide Mikuni on mine and I am jetted at a 180 main and needle clip on the richest setting.  Granted, i'm breaking it in still so really I should have a 175 in there and move the needle clip up a notch or two.  Still, START RICH.  Do your research.  There are many Polini tuning threads on MA that people have posted their jetting info on.

My favorite carb setup (this is my second Polini build) is the Dellorto 21mm PHBG with the intake that comes with the malossi reed block (you'll need a curvy piece of radiator hose to get it away from your head and out to the side).  There are other intake options available... Motomatic and Mike Thomas both make them.  You can also make your own.  The tecno circuit pipe, even though the headers ID is actually smaller than the ID of the exhaust port it still totally destroys.  On 16x40 gearing, stock timing and a tecno circuit you can hit 50mph NO PROBLEM.  My current setup is stock porting, TM24 with 180 main, timing @ 1.1mm BTDC, 18x40 gearing and a Simonini pipe... i'm hitting 55+.  Polini's actually like the timing being slightly retarded... some people run 1.6-1.8 but I find that too advanced and run too hot.  1.1mm is pretty close to 17deg, maybe a little less (correct me if i'm wrong here).

Polini's are very finicky, and if you do your research you'll hear that every time someone mentions the 64cc polini kit.  They love to seize and you must make sure to take it easy on the kit... run it rich, let it 4 stroke to high heaven... then slowly down jet.  They are, in my opinion, one of the best kits for Puch's besides the 74cc Gilardoni kit.  I like reeds... what can I say!

You can do some very simple porting modifications to the cylinder and hit 60+mph.  People port the intake windows 1-3mm (re-jet!) and widen the exhaust 1-2mm (even though the exhaust port is plenty big already) ... if you widen the exhaust you will probably lose a little compression so keep that in mind --- you may have to deck the head a little bit to re-gain compression.

so now I guess i'd like to hear anyones experience with this kit if they have it currently or have had it in the past.
