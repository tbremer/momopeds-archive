Scooter...

Don't buy Fuel line from Treats, goto your local Ace hardware and get some 5/16" OD x 3/16" ID Vinyl tubing, it'll run you roughly $.30 - $.50 /ft, I buy like 10 feet of it in the beginning of riding season, and I think I have like 3' left ish.

As for cleaning your tank, it's been covered many a time. If you're looking to get it just clean to test run your moped, I would do the following:

Drain gas.
Remove Petcock, Fuel Line, Gas Cap.
Buy The Works (it's like $2.50 for a quart, buy 2)
Block Fuel Outlet with something, I like Silicone Plugs or Caps.
Fill tank with said Work.
Shake tank and Bike once ever 5 - 7 minutes for about 45min - 1 Hour depending on how fuct yr tank is.
Drain the Works.
Fill tank with 1ga of HOT water/Baking Soda solution (2tbs / gallon of water)
Let set fooooor like..... I don't know 5 - 6 minutes.
Drain.
Fill tank with 1ga of gas-o-line swish around.
Drain.
Reassemble Petcock, Fuel Line, and Fuel Filter.
Fill tank to tippy-top-top with your choice of Premix.

Ride. Or test run. Or yeah.

Also, in all the downtime during this clean-up phase, it'd be good to clean your carb really throughly.

take that piece off the wheel and try to straighten it. use a vice and a hammer or pliers or whatever you gotta do. make it nice. then put it back on the wheel and put the brake plate where it's supposed to be. if you're having trouble getting the wheel to fit back in there between the swing arms, put one of your feet in between the arms and pull hard on the one closest to you to spread them out a little. while you got the wheel off, back off that cone nut a little and pack some grease in there for the bearings. you don't have to take the axle all the way off. when you tighten it, snug it up, then back it off slightly so that you don't feel a bind or any clicking. do that part real good. do it
