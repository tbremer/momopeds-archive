Repair and Performance / Re: How to seal a Malossi 4-petal Reed Block?
« on: July 01, 2010, 10:51:00 am »
make sure your mating surface is flat because its not and thats what is causing leaks. Properly setup no sealants are necessary. Gabe has a great guide:
http://magnumdash.wordpress.com/2010/03/18/guide-pro-tips-to-building-a-puch-polini/

To really make sure its flat just hold the intake up to the mating surface (towards a light source, BRIGHT) and tilt/rotate it until you can see light shining through. Get some files/sandpaper/whatever and work that until it is perfectly flat. Its really annoying to do because its usually not all the way flat (even when its close), but at that point once its nearly there the stock gaskets should be sufficient to seal it up.

Hardware isn't a huge deal, just make sure the piston slides through the cylinder with ZERO resistance with the intake tight. Since the mating surface on the cylinder isn't flat, don't worry, this happens to all of them, torquing the flat intake to the not flat surface warps the cylinder. So yea, if the piston snags when torqued, either continue flattening the cylinder and/or sand the piston down a tad on the intake so there is no resistance.

Hopefully all this makes sense.
