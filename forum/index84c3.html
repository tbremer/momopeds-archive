Repair and Performance / Pop Pop Sputter Pop.
« on: May 30, 2011, 03:06:13 pm »
You all may have recalled an earlier post where I described a sudden and contagious problem that started with my Grande.  I was riding it to work a month or two ago and it started to backfire, lose power and generally suck.  I limped it home and didn't ride it for a while.  I then broke out my Bravo and took it to work... and it did exactly the same thing.  I suspected that it may have been my pre-mix (winter) gas. 

Someone pointed out that I need to down-jet in the summer, so I down-jetted the 65cc Grande from 78 down to 72 (too much) and riding it, I got some more pops (louder and sharper) and I think I soft-seized it after one trip.  Now it runs until the engine gets hot, and then it just stops - can't pedal it or anything.  I haven't opened the motor up, so I'm not 100% sure yet... 

I bought the Bravo over to the fix-it on Sunday.  I cleaned the spark, drained the fuel and put fresh pre-mix in it before leaving and it actually ran fairly well all the way over to Mark's.  While there, I pulled the#49 Jet and Installed a #48.  The bike ran decent on the trip home, but I did get a couple light pops towards the end of the trip.  Obnoxiously, the spark is actually a very nice "coffee" color - which is supposed to indicate a proper A/F mixture... 

Today I tried to take the Bravo on a run to Lowe's.  I got nearly to carondelete park and got the same symptoms I had earlier in the year.  Pop, Pop, Sputter, Pop, no power.. death.  Had to limp it back home at about 18mph. 

I'm really unsure what's going on here.  clean sparkplugs, new gas, down-jetted.  Bike will idle just fine, but under load, and/or when the engine gets hot, it starts to run like shit. 
Both bikes ran awesomely in the fall/winter, but have been sucking pretty hard since March. 

Any ideas on what's happening here?  I'm sure I'll stumble onto it myself eventually, but any ideas that would speed up the process would be greatly appreciated.
