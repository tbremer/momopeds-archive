The reason I wanted to close off the stock reed intake and switch to piston port is because doing anything to the stock intake is extremely difficult because the carb and intake are squeezed in behind a drive belt.  I made a custom intake before to fit a 17mm phvg del on, so it is possible; however, using the intake on a Puch cylinder gains me access to a large variety of intake and carb options as well as exhaust choices. 

The reads are vertical and really small, the stock carb is only 12mm. 

As for porting the stock cylinder... Im up for that option although I have never done it before.   The stock cylinders are aluminum and I have a few lying around just waiting to get messed up.  But as Linda said.. its been done.

mines about half way done. Haven't decide about the reed block yet.

I got most of the spacing issues worked out, and need some time to think about reeds vs piston port, the reeds can ONLY be taken up to 15mm opening, which is great for stock (my mock up), but kind of shitty for a kit.

Oh, and the biggest issue to overcome is the fact that puch pistons are far too long on the skirts and hit the crank, so you really mess with your intake timing. they don't have 5mm worth of space to lower the intake, and cutting the skirt down leaves you with a massive intake duration. I'll officially measure it all this week, and tell you how long...

If you shaved the base down, you'd only uncover like half the exhaust port and none of the transfers at  BDC. Remember that for the most part, the top of your cylinder to the bottom of your exhaust port is roughly equal to your stroke. If you're shaving down the base, you never alter that dimension.
