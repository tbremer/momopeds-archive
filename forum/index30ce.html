your jetting most likely will change when you install the pipe.

You can order almost any size from TreatsHQ on eBay, or MopedJunkyard. I would check your jet and make sure all is clean, get a range of at least 2 under and 2 above. I would probably do more of a  high range if you're plannng to upgrade your bike anymore that.

On the other hand you could have an air leak, when the bike is runnng spray all of your seals (crank case, exhaust, intake, case halves, and base gasket)

4stroking and an air leak feel very similar and until you get to know your bike it might be hard to tell.

We have a box full of Bing jets at the compound, you might be able to fnd your ideal setting there.

Stock should be is 60 to 64 depending on your intake, carb, year, and where the bike was oroliginally sold.

The number wi be stamped on the jet

The four stroking got worse in the cold?


Good! You're either Jetted wrong or you have an Air Leak...

Lets try and clean the carb first...

To clean an Encarwi Carb you will need 1 Flathead Screwdriver, 1 9mm Socket, 1 11mm Socket and 1 can of Carb Cleaner.

On the Exhaust side of the bike there you'll see the engine, on the bottom is the carb, it's an Encarwi, and a piece of shit. There is a flat head screw that holds the carb onto the Intake Manifold, loosen it, take the carb off, and unscrew the Throttle Slide.

Also! The choke cable is on there, remove your Air Filter, there is a little spring arm that puts pressure on the cable, slide it over the choke and the cable will come out easily.

Your carb is now un-attatched!

Pull the jet holder out (Brass flat head screw -or 9mm socket- on the outside of the carb), fill a small container of Carb Cleaner, drop the jet and holder in there, let it set for a half hour.

Remove the Banjo (where  your fuel line attaches) (11mm Socket)  Pull it all apart, clean the filter with Canned/Pressurized Air (carb cleaner will ruin it!) , spray the banjo til carb cleaner comes out clean..

Remove the float cover, and float

Now! Clean all the gunk out of your carb, this may take a while, and a full can if cleaner, make sure everything spray through clean, especially the Venturi (it's a ceramic tube coming out of your float bowl and into where your slide rests)

Make sure your float isn't a sink (do you hear gas in it?) make sure the top of the float is 12.5mm from the top of the float needle (pointy side).

Once everything has been sprayed clean, dry it with a towel (lint free if possible! but not necessary) put the float/float needle back in (make sure the pin rests in the center holder), put the float cover back on (make sure the pointy side of the needle is up and slides into the center hole of your float cover (this stops the gas flow when the bowl is full).

Reassemble the banjo onto float cover,
put the jet back into the float bowl (if you have teflon tape you can wrap a turn and a half of tape on the threads of the Jet Holder, this will prevent leaking. Trim extra!),
reattach the choke assembly,
put the throttle slide back in (make sure the notch lines up),
put air cleaner back on,
attach carb back to the intake manifold
Tighten screw.

Reassemble!
