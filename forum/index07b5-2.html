Haha. Well, it's kind of a moot point, because any free time I have on my hands for the next 10 years will be spent scanning the remaining 40lbs of manuals I have. I kinda doubt I'm going to want to re-scan something when I could be scanning something new.

Even if something does print off a little fuzzy, I'm sure it's not the end of the world. People can always look at it on their computer if they need a higher quality reference. Just out of curiosity, will the page orientation make a difference? Like, when I scanned smaller manuals like the Korado manual, I did one landscape-oriented manual page per one landscape-oriented PDF page. I could probably figure out how to do two landscape-oriented manual pages on one portrait-oriented PDF page, which would make each image print smaller and probably make the printed versions less fuzzy (although they'd be smaller, so perhaps that cancels itself out).

The Photoshop stuff I'm doing repeatedly is rotating the images, cropping the images, straightening the images, and then usually messing around with contrast, brightness, levels, and auto-fix. I imagine that rotating and cropping could be done in batches, except that the rotation is alternated (one page needs to be rotated left, the next right, then left, etc), and because each page is a little bit different and gets placed on the scanner just a little bit differently, I'd be worried about blindly cropping everything to the same dimensions. Once I figure out how I can make the text and images look best on one image, I usually just do that same thing to all the other pages. I don't know how much of that can be batched, though, plus I've only got Elements, so something that may be able to be batched in one of the good versions of Photoshop might not work with my shitty version. I really don't mind spending the time on cleaning up the images so much; it's the scanning that's the real pain in the butt.

Quote
You need access to a nice, big, new copier that is networked and has an awesome scanner with an auto-document feeder.  They do awesome resolution at a page every two seconds or so.

That's pretty much what I'm looking into buying, except I want a home version instead of some giant, ungodly expensive office version. My top three right now based on features/reviews are the HP Officejet 4315, 4215, or J3680. All three seem to be about $100 used, have an auto-feeder, scan at a reasonably high resolution, and at least one of them claims to come with OCR software. I also already have a HP Printer, so I'd only have one printer manufacturer's package of extraneous bullshit software on my computer, rather than multiple sets of extraneous bullshit software from multiple printer manufacturers.

Quote
Yep, and I have one here at work. If your manuals are not bound, like in loose sheets I could scan them at 600/600 dpi in jpeg, tiff or pdf format in no time.

Most of the stuff in binders in this picture is loose sheets. (Admittedly, about 1/3 of that doesn't need to be scanned, either because it's a duplicate or because nobody gives a crap what the index number for a Trac carb screw was back in 1982).



Only things are, (1) a lot of the pages are torn or have worn edges, which might cause jams and related headaches, (2) a lot of the pages are an uncommon size (I think it's the European standard size?), (3) I put almost all of the loose pages in page protectors, and it will take a while to de-protector them for scanning, and (4) shipping would probably get expensive quickly. If you REALLY want to scan something, I'm sure I've got at least one or two that would scan nicely on an automated system without much babysitting, but I'd hate to rope up too much of other people's spare time with this. I'm sure it wouldn't be hard for most of you to think of something more exciting to do than scan moped manuals.

As far as the OCR stuff goes, I wonder if that's why I was having issues with it when I first tried it. I downloaded the free trial version of the ABBYY OCR software, and it was awful. Partly I just couldn't figure it out, partly the trial version wouldn't do what I wanted it to do, and partly it just just didn't work at all. I bet I was using a pretty low-res image, though. It would ultimately be nice to make the manuals searchable, but I would feel just a tiny bit bad about that. I don't know how many of you have seen, but 1977 got a bunch of manuals scanned a while back and they're online to download for $1 each (I'm pretty sure they've got more that they haven't put online yet too, but don't quote me on that.) Anyway, all of 1977's manuals are searchable. Free but non-searchable vs. $1 and searchable is a pretty fair toss-up. But if I offer for free something comparable to what they're selling, I'd feel like I was taking manual sales away from them. I think it was rad of them to get their manuals scanned and offer them to people for cheap, and I want to support that, not undercut it. It's something I'd have to give some more thought to and probably address on a manual-by-manual basis.

Yeah, I'm aware of potential copyright issues. For right now, I'm not too worried about it. The MRA (and several other sites) have been hosting manuals for YEARS, and it doesn't look like anyone has come after them for it. People have been selling paper and digital copies of all this stuff on Ebay for YEARS, and for all that it's "against Ebay policy" or whatever, nobody seems to have come after them for it. 75% of the brands I've got manuals for aren't around anymore, so the chances that whoever owns the rights for Trac is gonna come after me are pretty slim. As for the Honda, Yamaha, Suzuki, and Vespa stuff .... I guess I'll take my chances. As far as I know, none of the companies are still selling the manuals themselves, and it's not like I'm making any money by doing this. If they tell me to take it down, I will, but until then, it'll be there for whoever wants it (and after I take it down, there are always the ubiquitous illegal file-sharing sites). I've thought about trying to get permission to reproduce manuals from Honda/Yamaha/Suzuki, but I don't even know where I'd begin asking about that.
