Title: Best/Worst Job Thread
Post by: FakeDad on April 02, 2011, 02:36:34 am

Environmental Recycling Inc

8000 Hall St
Saint Louis, MO

Plastic Lumber Factory in North City, I lived about a block and a half away on Baden ave, in this fucked up apartment building peppered with real life gunfire. Me and my girlfriend at the time and the crack dealer across the hall were the only folks in the building that weren't devout crack-heads. My girlfriend was pretty crazy though, and had this idea in her head that I shouldn't be allowed to eat breakfast before I went to work at butt-fucking 6am, I think the idea was that I would lose weight, (which totally worked) but that was a pretty annoying start to the day.

When I showed up for work I chatted with the guys, most of which were thirty years older than me, some more. Not that it matters, but to help build the scene of the story, I noticed that I was one of three white dudes who worked there (other than the owner, Foreman, and some asshole who didn't seem to do anything but dick around in the office with the owner) One of the white dudes was named Carl, he was very short, very mustache'd and very pot-bellied. Carl always figured out a way to trick you into listening to his Viet-Nam stories, he loved to talk about Nam and it really annoyed me, I felt that he was way too  excited to see another white dude working at the factory. Race seemed to be a factor in who Carl would talk to, it wasn't for me, I was a damned dirty post-crust punk who was just trying to wrest some interesting story out of life. But anyway, fuck Carl, he worked on the other side of the factory anyway.

The other white dude was some asshole who always wore this fantastic "Too Blessed To be Stressed" sweatshirt, he always looked like he'd done meth for too long/drank himself to sleep every night, they only brought him in when too many people called out sick (which was like twice when I worked there).

The side of the factory I worked on housed the cutter/planer machine, I was the cutter half the time, other half I was the stacker. I was a terrible stacker, I fucked up alot, poor coordination, I was really slow at the task because I hated it, I hated putting myself in this menial position just to feel some bullshit "real life" experience. When I was the cutter I got to operate this really cool double blade saw, It had these really cheap plastic blade guards on it, and i got pretty good at just slamming my freshly cut plastic lumber into the side of the saw blade, fucking up the guard so that the Foreman would have to spend an hour fixing it while I took a standing nap or got high with the only other dude my age in some secret corner of the warehouse.

My two favorite guys were Anthony (the guy that cut when i stacked and stacked when i cut), a very charismatic dude who I later found out was also deployed to Veit-Nam, I had to pry this information out of him, when I asked him what it was like all he said was "It wasn't too nice, It wasn't too nice", Anthony had a shaved head and one gold front tooth, he was weary of me at first, that changed he saw me break the saw the first time, he really got to like me when I broke the factory implemented this "no smoking on the floor" rule. Anthony would let me drive the forklift sometimes, and I would abuse this privilage and race the damned thing around the floor, or drive to the other side of the factory, park it in the corner and get high.

Some insanely old guy named Tom (I think, let's just call him Tom) operated the planer part of the machine, he was also in Veit-Nam, never talked about it. Tom had a face like a leather purse, and the body frame of someone who had just been rescued from Dachau. He also grew to like me once he saw the weird shit I would do to get out of working.

This job was the worst I've ever had, the sheer physical duress, never being able to get all the little bits of plastic shit out of my hair, that and almost every night I would have a dream that I was still at the factory, cutting and stacking plastic lumber.

The best thing about the job was after Tom, Anthony and I all started getting along, It felt a little bit less like work, and alot more like three guys at the ends of their rope smoking cigarettes and breaking shit, and busting ass when we needed to.

My last day there, it was a Friday, I told Tom and Anthony that I was going on a two week camping trip, then leaving the country, and I'd probably never come back, they told me they'd believe it when they saw it/see you Monday.

I think about those dudes alot, I did leave the country, after a really weird-ass camping trip with my crazy girlfriend. I came back after dicking around Europe for a few months with her, but that's a shittier story.
