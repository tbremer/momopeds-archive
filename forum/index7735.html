Any time we have an unpopular war, there will be fewer people enlisting, so they have to offer greater incentives, money rank etc... Unfortunately, this means that the quality of recruits drops. What's the point? Well, the military operates the way it does because it has to. If it didn't, everyone would take adavantage of every loophole and nothing would get done. It's waht happens when you have an all-volunteer force that mostly, not all, joined up just for the money. You will do what you're told, no questions asked. It sucks, but it's a necessary evil, so to speak.


There are some pretty skeevy recruiters too, but in my opinion, in this day and age, information on what's true or not is easily attanable, so MOST people that have bad recruiters are as much to blame. I had a pretty crappy recruiter at first, telling me all kinds of crap I knew was only half-true, he didn't like me much. All it takes is a Google search here or there, or a quick conversation with a vet or someone who is currently serving.

Overall, my experiences were pretty positive. There's some stubborn ass-backwards shit going on, but you get that everywhere in life. The reason it seems worse in the military is because you can't say "I don't like this business, I'm finding another job." you're stuck until your contract is up.

I will say this, the Army, at least, is TOTALLY different now than it was 15 years ago. Basic training has been made a joke compared to my first time in. It's built to be extremely easy for people, and very few don't make it. Today's younger folks typically want things easy and quick, so the Army had to change basic to retain and pass through more of those types of people, a lot of guys my age who have served 15+ years resent that, understandably, because they see these young kids coming in who don't really care about anything but the paycheck and bonus, half-ass everything, and then give attitude about it. It's like night and day.

It's put the military in a tough situation, adn in the end, it's all about leadership. Hopefully we've turned a corner and things will improve.
