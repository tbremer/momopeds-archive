Title: Re: 2 Stroke Oil?? Whats The Skinny??
Post by: Motorcycle Justin on August 08, 2010, 08:51:25 pm
"Scientific reasoning", huh? Get into it man, this is the place for that. Why would you want to "hone out" your cylinder when you ride? I feel like that seriously reduces the life of your cylinder and rings. There's already a "little friction", actually a LOT of friction inherent in a running motor. There are also a lot of bad by-products of running shitty oil, like ring sticking and excessive carbon buildup (and Biery seizing his Airsal in Austin). Non-synthetic also tends to gum up components like power valves, we don't have those, but it does mean that all that shit gets pushed to your pipe. Also, I just feel it's a bit boorish to throw shit oil at nice components. Why half ass? It's these reasons that I run a good synthetic. It actually doesn't burn, so it stays suspended in the charge after combustion, and goes straight out the pipe because it's still atomized. I've never had to clean a piston due to carbon (and people like Mike P have picked on me because of how much oil I ran in the MK), and I've never had ring sticking problems.

Now, as for running non-synth during break-in, then switching back to syn afterwards.. I've never done this. I break in on the oil that I ride with. We can get in to break-in techniques, but mine have nothing to do with oil. Just a few heat/rpm cycles to get things mated and seated, then I ride it normally (tuning comes before riding, and I work a few heat/rpm cycles in when tuning). Again, never had any blowby or excessive wear issues. But let's not get into break-in, we can start another thread for that.

Title: Re: 2 Stroke Oil?? Whats The Skinny??
Post by: Motorcycle Justin on August 09, 2010, 01:33:56 pm
Stay away from castor because it's a non-synthetic bean oil. It's VERY VERY good as an oil, really good lubrication, good dwell time, AND you can mix it with alcohol (not that that matters much, some of the kart guys I ran with used castor, and they ran Ethanol). The thing is, it doesn't burn very clean. It leaves carbon gunk CAKED on your crown and head, and gums up rings really fast if you're not careful. The only reason the kart guys got away with it was because they did a full top end rebuild after every weekend, and replaced the rings every weekend. I really wouldn't use it on a moped, because, unlike a really high quality synthetic, it's overkill. You'd KNOW if you had an engine that required castor; which, in today's world of really good oils, there aren't that many that really do. I ran Motul Kart Gran Prix 2t, and never had wear issues compared to the castor guys, and didn't have to clean near as much at the end of the weekend. I've found that Castor is a REALLY good assembly oil, because it stays coated to components longer, good for that first start :)
