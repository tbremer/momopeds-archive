Some of those little 2 cycle cars were really neat.  For example, the 3 cylinder DKW had 3 coils lined up beside the engine head, with a wire from each one to its individual spark plug.  The Goggomobile coupe had a preselector electric transmission . . . with a little H-pattern switch on the dash, just select the next gear you wish to have, the tap the clutch pedal and it is instantly shifted (the would be nice on a dragster hotrod!!).  The Messershmitt  went in reverse by killing the engine and starting it up running backwards.  etc.  etc.

I've owned 3 Messerschmitts.  The last one I drove from Fort Dix, NJ to Little Rock, AR in 1969.  Mostly doing about 50 - 55 mph.  Top speed was about 70 mph but you couldn't do that for very long without it seizing.

Although it had the same 4 gears in reverse as in forward, the steering was to quick (1/4 turn lock to lock) that you would lose control and probably wreck if you wend any further than 2nd gear in reverse.

I also had many more of different makes of those little 1 and 2 cylinder cars back in the 1960s up to the 1980s when I collected them.
