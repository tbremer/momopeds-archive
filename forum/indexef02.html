DR is fast enough but thats not the first time I have heard of one failing. The price and performance is hard to beat otherwise.
Mallossi 43mm and Polini 43mm are both great quality and well performing-the polini seems to have better top end.  The alfere kit is an exact copy of the polini and is made in the same factory as the polini so it is a good deal.  I think the polini head is a nicer design than the mallossi-or mill down and match the squish band using a stock head.
The 38.4mm athena is really fun, loads of low/midrange power for a 50cc and it doesnt suffer the same poor reputation as the 43mm and 46mm versions.
The 43mm athena should be badass judging from my expirience with the 50cc version (I have a 70cc version to try soon:)  The quality of the athena is sort of a dissapointment but supposedly the major issues have been fixed.-I decided to try the 46mm since benji says the factory assured them that all previous issues have been fixed
The polini 42mm (with detacheable exhasut flange) is excellent performer and has excellent quality.

drill the intake to 13mm and cut the crank or buy a performance crank-the mazzi cranks seem to be good quality and decently priced-those two things really allowyou to breath-the rotary crank sort of induction on the vespas is really efficient, it is amazing what the 13.13 sha can do.

An after market vario makes a world of difference, along with or if you have to keep the stock a new contra spring.

meceur or mallossi race gears are really nice, the biggest benefit I have expirenced is drastically dropping the rpms at top speed makiing the bike more comfy to ride at topspeed

The directions that come w/ the polini kits actually give specs for cutting the crank, there are also some good threads on ciao.nl.  Cutting it it increases the intake duration-the cut out opening on the crank determines how long the intake charge is being pulled into the case.  You can open up the opening on the intake track too-but you are limited by the size of the track, you still need a good seal to be made.

I noticed in a thread trhat you are playing with a 207, I have been messing with a 1981 x30 with a 207 the past few weeks, opening up the exhaust port made a big difference (2mm on each side).  I have an intake and pipe coming tommorow.
