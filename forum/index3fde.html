« on: July 04, 2011, 11:39:09 pm »
I have just acquired a minty 1982 Suzuki FA50, and I've been reading on MA about performance options. Right now it is bone stock, and goes around 28mph. This is going to be for my mom, and she's a bit heavier than I am, and she wants it to be a daily driver. She/I want it to look as stock as possible and ride it as much as possible, while also keeping it as reliable as I know FAs are.

I had a 87 FA50, and I got lucky. 35mph stock w/out upgrades on the flats

Here is my list of things to get her up to speed:

1: clean the shit out of the pipe

2: remove the oil injectors drive shaft (so it looks stock but isn't)

3: replace spark plug

4: inflate tires to max

5: get a better shock

6: Mill down the head

7: port match

If need be:

8: Slap a Simo pipe on it

9: Slap a bigger carb on it

10: Look for FZ50 kits... (same stroke as FA50s)

Has anyone dealt with the anguish of slow nopeds before? Does anyone have any tips/info/querries about my TO DO list? Is there anything I'm missing?
