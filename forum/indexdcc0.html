She's alive!  Kinda.  I wore myself out trying to pedal-start it and confirm fuel, fire and compression before I realized that I had reversed the ignition switch and headlight wires.

Were this a car, I'd strongly suspect that the carb is out of tune.  Too weak, maybe an empty fuel reservoir...  I know SU carbs pretty well, but know very little about the Del'orto carbs.  I did disassemble and clean the carb before I put the bike together it seems simple-enough but you never know.  While I have a spare that I can tinker with, I really know nothing about how to tune this sort of carb.  I did not see a mixture adjustment at all.  The idle seems to be adjustable with the cable/throttle apparatus.  Beyond that...?

Can anyone point me to an info source on these?

I did a compression test.  My cylinder has 110 psi of compression.  Normal?  A little low for a car engine, but here that may be fine.

I spent a little more time with the bike this afternoon.  I carefully disassembled and cleaned my other spare Dellorto (another 12.10 I got complete on eBay a couple weeks ago).  This 'other' carb has some varnish on inside, but was otherwise in much better condition than the original bravo carb or the ciao carb I had already tried. 

After sealing the float bowl gasket and replacing the cruddy jet, I installed the whole thing with a refurbished air filter box and fired it up. 

She idled well.. She reved well.  She drove well.  She stopped well.  I ran it around the empty lot next door.  Ran it home, ran it down the long alley and back up.  It did not miss a beat. 

I was getting some white smoke out the tailpipe.  I'm not sure if that's normal or not.
