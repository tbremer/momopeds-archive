Stay away from castor because it's a non-synthetic bean oil. It's VERY VERY good as an oil, really good lubrication, good dwell time, AND you can mix it with alcohol (not that that matters much, some of the kart guys I ran with used castor, and they ran Ethanol). The thing is, it doesn't burn very clean. It leaves carbon gunk CAKED on your crown and head, and gums up rings really fast if you're not careful. The only reason the kart guys got away with it was because they did a full top end rebuild after every weekend, and replaced the rings every weekend. I really wouldn't use it on a moped, because, unlike a really high quality synthetic, it's overkill. You'd KNOW if you had an engine that required castor; which, in today's world of really good oils, there aren't that many that really do. I ran Motul Kart Gran Prix 2t, and never had wear issues compared to the castor guys, and didn't have to clean near as much at the end of the weekend. I've found that Castor is a REALLY good assembly oil, because it stays coated to components longer, good for that first start
