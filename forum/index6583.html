Start by rebuilding your engine and replace the crank while you are at it, they aren't really expensive and if you want to make it high revving, the stock crank isn't going to cut it.  You can start with a pipe, but if you want to go fast, you are going to eventually change out the cylinder, head, carb, and probably the clutch.  A CDI is nice as well, but you can do just fine on points.

The reliability of the bike is really up to how you build it.  If you take your time and make sure to do a thorough job, you will only need to do basic maintenance like oiling your chain and tightening bolts down until something catastrophically fails.  A lot of parts are generally not awesome in the quality department, so be prepared to replace things that break from time to time.  You will probably destroy some major components if you like to ride hard, but that's all part of the game.

So maybe someone else here (Beez?) knows this and I'm just ignorant, but do you have an E50 (single speed) or ZA50 (two speed) on there? That will make a difference in what we suggest.

That being said, as general advice, I'd probably recommend a kit, given your circumstances and what you're looking for. Can you go fast on a stock cylinder with the correct setup? Yeah. But if you want it to be "reliable" transportation and be able to keep up with traffic, both up top and off the line, then I'm still leaning towards telling you to kit it.

Cons:
As Brian said, you will lose fuel economy though. I think the average stock fuel economy is about 100mpg, which comes out to what, like 75 miles for a full Maxi tank? With a larger carb and average kit setup, I'd guess more like 50-60mpg, so ... well, someone else can do the math on that. 50mpg is still really good, but it does get sucked up fast when your tank doesn't even hold a gallon. If you get a kit and stay with a 14 or 15mm carb, I think that might help improve fuel economy a little, but see if anyone else can corroborate that.

Also, about the whole "reliability" thing... Mopeds break. A lot. When you put performance parts on them, they break even more, plus it's usually more expensive to fix whatever broke. I think both Mike and Brian covered the reliability topic and how to improve reliability when possible, so I don't have much to add there. You ought to check out the stuff on MA too though, just to see what all is out there -- //www.mopedarmy.com/wiki/Category:Performance_Tuning

being that said, the idea here is two levels one top and one on the bottom the top side of the intake is for what real rodders call the primary side of the carb. that the front two barrels of a fore barrel carb. that right i said four of what a moped has, but way bigger. now, the first two barrels do feed all 8 cyl. and once again that's 8 of what a moped has. and still bigger one again. now the purpose for this is to limit the intake volume and speed up intake velocity. this give a better low rpm power or bottom end torque. pulling power!!!  and only using the front two barrels of the fore barrels it has. means better fuel economy, for that power. now the second side of the duel plane intake is made for wot ( that's "wide open throttle" ).  the second two barrels offer more power, but for the top end of the rpm band. that is till the cam runs out of room in its own designed grind of cam lobe. duration and lift. ok, the duel part of the intake is the part that those back side barrels are for it increases the intake volume letting the air flow easier, more freely. well it doesn't matter if its 2 cycle or 4, more air means more power. so long as the air fuel ratio is maintained. and being able to get more air in the cylinder, is called volumetric efficiency. so now u have the fundamentals of a duel plane intake. you may gather that a single plane intake has way more volume in the plenum area. that the area under the carb. in a duel plane intake there's a septum. a divider like your nose. there can be two or fore openings on an duel plane intake. now the single plane intake only has one very large, deep opening. and the sacrifices low end power or torque. but offers way more top end rpm power, over the duel plane intake. but you have to have a cam that gives you the rpm availability. to flow as much as the intake can offer.  that's the differences of a duel plane and single plane intakes. that's intakes 101. any thing else?? so as you may gather, 2cycle or 4. you make trade-offs. speed or economy, with the alterations you make. but sometimes you can happen upon a freak of nature, were the combo package is better that the original engineered design. once again, thanks for the info.

Not a lot of the info translates between 2 and 4 strokes, for instance the free flowing air=more power is not true in the 2 stroke. Controlled air=more power in 2 strokes. A free flowing exhaust on a 4 stroke is good, a free flowing exhaust on a 2 stroke will cause a blown engine. Very small changes can have major effects on these engines for better or worse. Many of these changes can be made with a dremel or file. Talk to those that blast you ( pass you at a high rate of speed )on a ride, learn from him or her, do your own research and prove your understanding by blasting them. The moped wiki is a great place to start and diverge from there for more secrets of this dark art.
