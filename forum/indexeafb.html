Ha, Ed, your other post got me wanting to look into this more. The last thing I wanted to look up for us was license plate lights. The closest I can find is Chapter 8, Article 17, Statue 6, which says that motor vehicles need tail lamps, blah, blah, and "Either a tail lamp or a separate lamp shall be so constructed and placed as to illuminate with a white light the rear registration plate and render it clearly legible from a distance of fifty (50) feet to the rear. Any tail lamp or tail lamps, together with any separate lamp or lamps for illuminating the rear registration plate, shall be so wired as to be lighted whenever the head lamps or auxiliary driving lamps are lighted." But again, we're not motor vehicles. There's a very similar version of this in Article 18 (for required equipment on motorcycles), but that doesn't apply to us either, since we're not motorcycles or motor-driven cycles. I mean, I might be missing something, but from what I've just read, it sounds like they can't get us for having non-illuminated plates either.

Also, 8-1703 says "Motorcycles, motor-driven cycles and motorized bicycles manufactured after January 1, 1978, shall display lighted head and tail lights at all times that such vehicles are operated on any highway." Maybe this is what Beez was talking about with the 1978 thing. This says that we DO need tail lights, unless your moped is registered as something 1977 or earlier.
