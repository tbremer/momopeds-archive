Mopeds (under 50cc pedal assists two wheel vehicles) are decades old pieces of technologically decrepit machinery. They are neither reliable nor maintenance free. They will break down on you at very inconvenient times, will leak oil and probably gas. They will need frequent spark plug changes, exhaust cleaning and many other periodic maintenance items. Mopeds should not under any circumstances be considered a viable alternative to a brand new modern scooter. They usually have a top speed of less than 25 miles per hour on a flat street on a good day with a decent tail-wind.

That guy posts on MA. I think he's alright -- he posted an explanation for why that's on his site a while back, and it made sense. People come into his shop and see mopeds and go "Aww, how cute! I have to have one!" or they see that a moped is $500 and a scooter is $1500, and they want the moped just because it's cheaper. That warning is to try to scare those people away, because the moped will break, and then they'll be unhappy with it. Mopeds really are better for people who like tinkering or want to learn.
