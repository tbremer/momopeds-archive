Nice topic.

Back when I was in 7th grade, I guess 8 or 9 years ago, my brother's friend rode over to our house on his yamaha chappy. The thing was beautifully slow and unreliable, and I thought it was amazing. He rode it around for a few days before it broke down on him and no one knew how to fix it. Fast forward to 2006 and a few friends were trying to fix the chappy again with no results. Meanwhile, another close friend picked up a sweet sparta foxi after he and another friend had been riding their minibikes around town for awhile. He obviously wanted to make it faster, and I watched all this happen remembering the chappy in my friend's garage. I wanted to get it running, but had no idea what to do. Another friend got a Demm Smily and the chappy was going to get thrown away. I rescued it and began tinkering with it. I'm still not sure exactly how I got it running, but it involved a propane torch and a lot of pedaling. Once running, the rest is history. Now I kind of know what I'm doing which is nice.

The first moped I bought was a Peugeot 102SP that I paid $500 for in Denver because I didn't know better and it was beautiful and ran.  Then it stopped running and at some point it had a "fuel tank" leak which I promptly repaired with JB weld and then sold to Beth... and now it is a bait bike at The Fort.  I found that one on craigslist and thought it'd be pretty fun to have a moped.  It broke down a lot and I remember one time someone mentioning "Johno can fix mopeds, take it to him".  I was still new to Denver so I had a hard time finding him but after I sold it I eventually moved out to NE Denver and actually ended up living 3 blocks from him, his wife, another guy and Nathan Pelak.  Nate would come over and hang out with us (punks!) and he mentioned to me that Johno had a moped he could sell me and to call him.  I called him and he told me he had a Hobbit that he could put back together for $400 and so there began my first real moped experience.  I eventually stripped out the case on the intake side and gave up and then got a Milani (V1 powered)... yup.  I learned what I know mostly from Johno and Nate because they were patient with me and let me hang out at the shop constantly and watch and ask questions until I started feeling comfortable doing the work myself.

I was deprived as a child as most 50's kids were only we didnt know we were.  No motorized anything, I had a western flyer single gear bike that I got around on till 14yrs. old.  It was always a dream to someday get a minibike which never happened.  My lifestyle never supported buying or collecting worldly items.  I never owned a stick of furniture till I was 30 being I was a road scholar and lived in rented furnished places or lived in hotel/motels.  Then when the world brought me to my senses and I got a real job and wife and kids the collecting began, frig, couch, bed, house etc.  As time went on my job was 7 miles away and I thought it would be good for me to get a moped type thing to ride to work since the speed limit was 30-35  all the way to work at 4:30 in the morning.  The slow pace of it compared to the busy, noisy, super fast pace work I did it was appealing to slow down when I wasn't in the hell hole.  Well I finally got a yamahopper but unfortunately my job after 28 yrs. had already disappeared.  The pace of the moped/noped has caught up with me and I LOVE IT!!!  happy  ending.
