Re: Forced Air Cooled 103
« Reply #31 on: August 02, 2011, 11:53:33 am »
Ideally you would want the scoop to have a larger cross sectional area than the motor you are trying to cool.  So if the frontal area of the cylinder is 100cm squared then shoot for 150 or 200 cm squared for the scoop front.   As the air passes by the engine, giving a little room for blow by around the fins will allow for plenty of flow without needing too much pressure.   I would just measure the fins and give 1/2 that around the engine for airflow.  So if the fins are 10mm deep, I would make the scoop sit 5mm away from the engine fins. 

An aluminum shroud/ scoop will transfer more heat than a glass one.  It will transfer radiant heat  where it is close to the block and give a larger surface area for dissipation.  If you wanted to really step it up, add cooling fins to the shroud too.
