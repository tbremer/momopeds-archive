Quote from: Tom! on February 08, 2010, 02:00:47 am
So, I know it doesn't say, but how long after running a piston do you think this would be advisable to pull open and look at?
Depends on the type of oil you run. If you run non-synthetic, it's been my experience that the wash/burn patterns show up a lot sooner than with, say, Motul 800. I ran my bike for a whiiiiile (45mm DMP) on Motul, ran it pretty lean for max powuhhh, and for the longest time only had a bit of varnish on the top. It only started showing burn patterns after we went to Denver and I was running way too rich.

Quote from: Tom! on February 08, 2010, 02:00:47 am
And, like plug chopping, after you pull a piston, should you clean it totally off so you can re-read it again later?
I wouldn't use this as quite the accurate tuning method like plug chops are. It's more of a long-term "how am I running, really", just like looking at the underside of the piston crown for burnt oil. Clean your piston as often as you usually would to remove carbon and such, if you have such problems. I try to rebuild/clean, at most, every 50 hours of riding.
