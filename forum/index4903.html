Vespa Piaggio Super Deluxe - No Spark
« on: April 05, 2010, 04:25:38 pm »
I've got a full-fledged 1978 Super-Deluxe Bravo, with the extra winding in the engine case, a 6v battery, and original stock turn-signals.  I've been collecting parts and re-assembling it since last year.  Aside from the proper ground-point run switch, I've completely re-assembled it.

I know you guys mostly avoid the painstaking restorations for a moped and usually eschew turn signals in favor of "go-fast" parts, but I wanted an ultra-safe signal-equipped cruiser for my daily commute. 

I tracked down a lot of little parts, rebuilt the motor, and I finally put the whole thing back together last weekend after re-soldering and replaced damaged portions of the the wiring harness. 

I've got the uber-cool Vespa Moped manual from ZippyMopeds and followed the wiring diagram very closely.  I sourced the proper 6v battery, purchased 32oz of the highly-toxic battery acid, charged the battery up, wired up a temporary ground/run switch  and tried to start it up....

I got no spark.  While pedaling I get headlights when the headlight bucket switch is on, but I get no power at all going to the coil and obviously no spark at the plug.  Tried using my multimeter and got no reading at all from my purple ignition wire. 

I traced the hot wire from the coil wire up to the headlight switch assembly and back to the external condensor and back inside the engine case. 

Checked points gap too. 

I'm guessing a wire inside the case is frayed out, or the condensor is bad.  I know how to check resistance, but how do you diagnose a bad condensor? 

Aside from not getting the wiring right, is there something else I could be overlooking?
