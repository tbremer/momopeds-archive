Ayrab-where do you live?

You need to get that pipe un-bracketed from where it is because the part of the frame t6he "Swingarm" moves up and down with your rear suspension, so if you hit a nice bumpe, which you will, your pipe will either snap in the middle or worse, you could damage your motor where it attaches to the pipe.

Get some new fuel line. Try Ace Hardware or another mower or motorcycle shop, and a new filter as well.

Is your fuel tank rusty>?

Remove the gas cap and gert yourself a flashlight.

Dump ALL the fuel in there, you don't know what's in it or how long it's been there, check tank, if not rusty, then flush with some gas, put lines back on and filler up.

Replace you spark plug, ride for a bit, then "plug chop" this will tell you a lot about how your motor is running.

SHIT! Wait, before you go, there's a little plastic plug in the side of your transmission, remove it and check your Transmission fluid, if low, fill.

Do NOT remove the small bolt on the bottom of your motor, it isn't a drain plug!

Cheap and easy way to get the rust out:

Get a bunch of nuts and bolts and metal shit. BBs work too. Throw them in your tank. Tape up any holes where they could get out.

Put the tank in a small box and pack it with some paper or other packing material. Tape the box shut.

Put the box in your dryer (or do it at the laundromat). Pack all around it with blankets or sleeping bags or other soft stuff.

Put the dryer on cold air and run it for an hour or two.

Pull the tank out and rinse it out really good with water.

Done.
