the ZA is in KC now and has been since last fall, Rick sold the bike to me and I TOLD him I would be changing it and he said fine, just offer the exhaust to him first if I were to sell. I offered Rick his exhaust back months ago when I considered changing the bike or selling it after I was done. and he never got back to me. I made sure to let him know I was considering selling it way back when I was still getting the bike cleaned for paint.

The only things that were still on it from rick was pretty much the top end. and even at that, the only custom part was the exhaust. It was my bike at this point. I made it mine. so I traded it for something I wanted to build next.

I dont regret it at all and its pathetically sad how much you're going on about it. It really sealed the deal with me being sick and tired of everyone trying to change my ideas on my things. If I go to any other forum about a project, be it the VW I helped a friend finish a few years ago, the RC cars I used to build and race, or now the motorcycle forums for this bike. NONE of them give me as much elitist griefer crap as some of you guys have. thats the honest truth and why I didnt come to the STL rally. my brother and I were going to bring both running bikes and ride and everything. bring out some good food and whatnot. but after all the grief, I didnt want to. The only people who I still look forward to talking to about anything are linda, beez, johnrob, and a couple of the other mid-mo dudes. most of the KC people I dont know and never met, and some of the STL people were cool. but theres too much grief here for me to want to stay. I'm not a hipster, I'm 21 years old, a student, and always looking for the next challenge.

Do you want to expand the hobby or narrow it down? Being open to ideas and people of any background and status will draw people to you and make them want to do things with you. I enjoyed being on your forums when I was looking for people to ride with and talk to about a common ground, but since I was added to the actual ?rosters? its been bittersweet. I went from being excited about all of this, talking to you guys, seeing your projects and what you do with them, but then it turned into a giant e-peen contest. Who had the ?better bike? and ?more moped-y moped?. Faster, shinier, better, cleaner, kitted, brand parts, racer look. The bike was becoming a status symbol for me and I wanted it to just be a bike that I felt good about and  liked to ride.

It was a "sweet moped" because I made it that way with my time, detail, and energy. If you had been here right by my side on the build, I would feel differently, but when I built it, I was by myself and I preferred it that way because I learned a lot from it and I'm ready for another project. Johnrob is really the only one I think that should get any say if any. He helped me out when I needed fenders cut and some advice or ideas. When I was getting ready for paint, scooter really helped me out with suggestions and links to procedure. I thank both of you for that and anyone thats been civil and accepting.

But what you are forgetting is that this was MY bike. Paid for, built, thought up, and created by me. Making it mine to do what I want with. If I had jetted wrong and blown up the kit, I know some of you would be right there saying something about how I ?deserve a medal/trophy for blah blah blah? but I didnt. I was careful, always re-checked things I knew would settle in and kept it running great for a few hundred miles and it ran better than ever when I traded it. But after all the grief I got about it before, during, and after the build, all it had become to me was an object that made me think of how un-accepting you guys have been and how I wanted to just be done. So I traded it away.

Thats the story. I traded a non-titled puch maxi for a titled kawasaki motorcycle. straight trade, no cash or anything extra and i have the title in hand.
