I haven't ridden my puch since about the MidMO Rally last year because the carburetor is doing this:

It runs fine for a few seconds and then dies.  However, if you push down the little primer rod (that pushes down on the float to open the needle valve to let the bowl fill) it will run.  That is, it'll run while you're holding the valve open and then start to die a bit after you let it close.  You can keep it running (with gas pouring out from around the top of the rod pushbutton) by working this valve as needed (pretty much a constant "fiddling with it" type of thing).

I figure I've confused everyone by trying to explain it...so I'll clarify if need be.

Anyway...I THOROUGHLY cleaned the carburetor recently, put it all back together, put it on the bike and guess what.  It's doing the same damn thing.  Talk about frustrating.

So, it sounds like a classic case of a too-high float level which is shutting the neddle valve before the carb can get enough fuel for the engine to run on.  Starvation.  "Sounds like" is the key here because the thing is, the float level has never been changed...but the problem just came up one day.

I'm wary of adjusting the float level, because I figure this is some other problem.

Does anyone have any ideas?

For the record, the float DOES float...I checked it.

Local Discussion (MidMopeds) / Invitation to "Back 40 Racing" September 20th, 2009
« on: September 09, 2009, 12:09:14 pm »
Hey all,

Here's your invitation to come out and experience some unbelievable fun on Sunday, September 20th around noon (back 40 time is much like moped time, it seems).

What it is:  A 40 acre parcel with a 20 acre pasture and 900 acres of forest (give or take...I've got not time for math!).  On the pasture is a 3/4 mile long rally-type road course (just a worn-in groove).  Every now and then, some of us get together and race junk cars on said course.  If you want to drive, bring a junky car.  Don't expect it to be roadworthy again after participating in Back 40 racing.  If you don't bring a car, plan to watch and/or hop into one of the backseats to experience the thrill. 

We also have some 4x4 trails cut out in the woods for jeeps, atvs, dirtbikes, etc.  Bring 'em.  There will be Jeeps, Samurais, Gator-type-thingies, and more I'm sure.

Where it is is 8750 Ball Road, Centralia MO.  Google be buddy buddy wit you.

Who is invited.  Anyone who's not an asshat that might get drunk and/or hurt and/or sue someone else (I'm the obvious exception).  And just in case you do...know ahead of time that nobody has any real worth...so good luck with that.  But yeah...really.  We do this for fun and the owner of the land likes it and lets us do it (and participates).  We don't want to spoil it by having someone so much as sprain an ankle.  I'm making this public, and I trust that whoever you bring with you will be as good of folk as the rest of us.

I'll get back here when I'm not at work and post a couple of videos that will either scare you FAR away from this sort of jackassery, or, and I think this is more likely...make you drool and sleep bad for the next week and a half as you anticipate being in a spot full of awesome.

What to expect: 
Fun.  I try, but words just cannot express how much fun it is to drive around at speed rubbing door handles (literally) with the car next to you. 
Bugs and snakes and mice and such.  For some reason, wasps and mice think back 40 cars make excellent domiciles.   
Expect it to be a long day.

What to bring:  Think of it like camping.  Bring some food and water (though you can always hike back up to the spiggot to fill up your canteen), some sunscreen and bugspray, some shade if you want it (EZ up canopies ROCK!).  A place to sit (lawn chairs and such).  We'd appreciate a gallon or a few of gas if you want to bring up a can of it.  It's not like we can just run up to the gas station to fill 'em up...and we'll burn plenty during the day.  We never have enough batteries to get all the cars going.  if you've got a spare GOOD automotive battery...tote it along.  We might use it for the day and you can take it home when you go.

What not to expect: 
Serious competition.  We do this for fun. 
Jerks.  again, we do this for fun and nobody gets too upset. 
Fame and fortune.  Duh.
Bathrooms.  It's the country, you're going to be peeing wherever you feel most comfortable (trees, weeds, from the top of one of the cars...).

And yes...we can race mopeds.

Crazy Clem Sparks
(I might seem laid back...but wait 'til you see me in the Back 40)
