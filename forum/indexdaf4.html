flat reed is 40mm and you can make sachs kits fit them with alot of mods. but why do that when you can get a  derbi 75 metra for it and destroy any kit across the board. puch and tomos kits can go on PP and PR engines but you always have the 3rd transfer to complicate thigns on the PR. as far and im concerned the stock jugs are great and need no mods done to them, im hitting 45 mph on an unported stock PP cylinder! (prob more like 43 since i havent gpsed it and my speedo says 45) kits are out there you just have to look hard.
if you kit your PR or PP engine its a good idea to look into regearing. tomos rear sprockets can easily be modded to fit. you cang o as low as 21 to keep your bike from rapping out to high. stock gearing is 19 X 30 and you can change to 19 X 21.
flat reed gearing is stock 13 X 30 with exception to the ds50 which is 17 X 30.
im enjoying my stock (3 roller) varo right now and am having weights machined for it. the tjt is good on derbis and i feel like it fits way better then on a hobbit (more solid) but its rumored that the derbi 6 roller is the best. ive rode all setups and a tuned derbi 6 roller (come stock FR engines) on a tuned kitted flatreed hits probably 1 and a half times harder than a tuned and kitted hobbit.  i would recommend mild clutch springs like polini 1500s so you dont have to relace youre clutch once a year.
