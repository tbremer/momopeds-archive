If you're really after 1mph/cc, you're probably going to have to revise your setup a LOT. After you get your tuning legs with the Gila, I'd suggest at least the following:

Tuning - get out of the "area under the curve" (widest and flattest powerband) mindset. With a single speed, unfortunately, you WILL have to sacrifice rideability to get a certain desired top speed, specifically 70+. You'll have to "suffer" with a peaky setup. The upside is that you <i>can</i> gear down once you've hit that magic number, still go 50+ and it will be VERY rideable.

VForce Reed Block - 6 pedal, gives you a lot more headroom with rpm, especially with the Gila. Usually good for +1-2hp on setups such as this.

Pipe - no matter what you do, you're not going to get the kind of performance you'd need with anything resembling a stock Techno Circuit. No, you won't have the low-end torque, and no, it won't <i>restrict</i> your cylinder, but it also won't help it on the top end; and help on the top end is what you really need. You'll need a MUCH larger header, something at least 28+mm. You'd be way better off with a Boss, but I'd suggest something dimensionally similar to a 6p/8p/Motomatic N8P. No destroyers, you need more headroom than even those offer. You'll probably have to modify one of those pipes as well, I can say that the stinger diameter won't be optimal. That's just something you'll have to adjust after doing a hard run and watching your head/exh temp.

Gearing - You're geared WAY too high for that cylinder. The Gila port map is optimized for around 11k rpm, then depending on the pipe it will taper, but will still rev to 13k, assuming everything else is tuned correctly. If top speed is ALL you're after, start around 18x36, which is a bit lower than your 18x35, and even still, you won't be getting on to the pipe until 50+.

Instrumentation - Something SEVERELY under-implemented in the moped community. At the bare minimum you'll need head temp and exhaust temp. Head temp gauges are all over the place, exhaust temp you'll have to make yourself. You can find probes designed for two-stroke race karts (search for Digatron). Tune for head temp between 380-420 (unless you do certain gas porting mods), and an EGT of NO MORE THAN 1200C. This is also an easy way to prevent seizes, if you see your EGT spike, pull your choke and let off the throttle. This is where a remote cable choke comes in handy.

You WON'T, at least not on an E50, be able to make a setup that goes 70+ and be able to ride it daily, unless you're okay with 5hr service intervals. I operated my 60mph MK80 on 10-hr service intervals, which ended up being about once a week, and I didn't ride every day.

Yeah man, that's totally doable. Do a couple runs just to say you've gone fast, then gear down. That's what I did with the Metra. The only time I ever GPS'd it was after I geared down and it was right at 60, but was one of the fastest bikes at our rally off the line. That cylinder/motor is doing work in Kenden's bike right now, performs pretty much the same - in fact, I wish he'd gear it WAY up just to see where it finally runs out of steam. I think your setup, as it sits, is probably good for 56-60ish. You're just geared too high. At that speed you'll be around 9k rpm, which is above the pipe's tuned range, and below the tuned range of your cylinder porting. You'll hit a real flat spot in your powerband and I'd think that's where your speed will kinda settle off. You also won't have great off-the-line performance either. I'd recommend going down to 17x35 for long stretches or 16x35 for around town. That'll put you at 9750 or 10500 rpm at 60, respectively. Don't worry about running the higher rpm, your parts are designed to do this. You'll have WAY better off the line performance, and you'll get a taste of what the Gila is designed to do - hi hi rpm.

A stock E50 will run to around 7-8k rpm. Your race crank is good to 15k, and with the 43mm Puch stroke (I calculated this out a few months ago), pistons should, depending on silicone content, be good to around 16,000 +- 1000. So I'd say the DANGER ZONE for any of the commercially available parts is 15k, as long as you're running a good oil. Ester- or castor-based only plz. For hi rpm builds, Ester-based should be 30:1, castor-based should be 25:1 unless your kit mfr says otherwise (Polini)
