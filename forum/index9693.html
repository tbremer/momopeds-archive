The timing should be perfect as is.  Running it advanced, but it didn't like running at the factory settings (but my setup ain't factory - so that makes sense).  Point gap is to specs.

Mine will run fine sans taillight.  I haven't had one hooked up for weeks, and this issue just started on the last Thursday night ride I went on.  Only some models/years of Puchs were wired that way.

Continuity would be hard to test, since it's intermittent.  It would show continuity most of the time, then fail for a spark or 3, then pick back up.  It's fun when that happens around 45+mph.  I feel like a real rodeo cowboy.

You know how the wire threads into that pokey deal on the coil, and a similar one of the spark plug cap?  I think the wire itself is kinda stretched out so there's not a real good connection.  That's my theory.  I'm gonna try the new cable and see what happens.  If it doesn't work, at least I will have taken one variable out of the equation.
