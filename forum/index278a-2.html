These colors rock,  Kaylese studied colors among other arts as a BFA in school. We did not go out of our way to spend time and money to create something you have seen before. Its a girls bike. I think its absolutely beautiful. These colors do require you to have an artist palette to be in Aww!. For others it will grow on you. This color collaboration is of European heritage. There is an Indy car with these exact colors.  I typed race cars powder blue and green, and an Indy car came up with the exact colors, keep in mind it was after we built the bike. We did not copy the car. Not that it matters. I hope this answers your question Justen.

 I also saw cloths shoes another car, and tons of shit, type powder blue and green and press enter just Google it. 

Tom, im not sure the total, the wheels cost $50 to powder coat and then like $30 for the powder so $80 Total there


So the rest of the bike was about $60 to powder coat and the light blue powder cost $10 = 70 + 80 = $150 remember thats for a custom double coat on the wheels too. The wheels had Ultimate Chrome as a base and then star bright green you cant see it in the picts but its sparkles really bright. Also she had all her little stuff powder coated so when you take off her side panels you will see a metallic clutch and a metallic variator.

I had to break it down cause i also had him do another pair of forks and my Buell foot pegs all four black plus the shifter and shifter linkage and brake pedal. All together it cost $185 + 40 for powder / he supplied the black / = $225 for all three bikes.
  On my ciao i had the forks, gas cap, rear variated clutch, variator. So you see it was a great deal!

 We got the bike as a donor bike from Jack attack so we had no investment our theory on this project was we can collect 10 mopeds and have half or more of them not run and take up space. Then the rest would spread us thin, cause we would do a new pipe here and a variator there and a new tire over there, you get the point. Fuck that, we decided to keep two stock and build two all out dream bikes one each. Plus we would help each other by doing it this way there would be full on attention from two people on to one machine. It would bring us closer plus i can teach her some stuff and learn some stuff about art. With building this bike when she goes to work we stop and i wont touch i until she gets back so she can be apart of the whole build. She wants to build her own bike. I think thats cool because she could have had me build it for her.
 So back to my point Tom the money we spent on powder coating was justified #1 because the bike was free, #2 Kaylese plans to keep her custom bravo for ever. #3 You cant put a price on Art. #4 More then anything this build has brought us closer together. I think im going to cry. Ha! #5 It is very pretty! OMG!! LOOK At IT!!  Unless your Opie from the stix you will appreciate the colors. I cant stop looking at it. Its just an absolutely amazing choice of colors. I'm proud of her.

Ray took the lower motor cage apart and he powder coated the kick stand, spring, and cage all separate, then put it back together. He does all the sand blasting and provides all the heat tape, he preps all the pieces and deals with all the screw holes and bearing holes etc. He knows his shit. You just drop it off and talk to him for 5 min, to go over the details of what you want and thats it. He calls and you get a smile on your face and you get all excited to go pick it up. While your there you can walk next door and go see Scooter, hes out there working all the time. His garage is side by side to Rays. 15 feet apart. My bike is next.
 
 So if anyone is concerned about getting powder coat done have no fear its opens doors to another world of possibility to what your moped can be if you were thinking about a change in color. Its like WOW! Hello! Color is wonder and a gift to explore. If you are not sure about colors consult an expert. Also for $1 a piece you can get sample panels mailed to you from powderbuythepound. So you can see exactly what a particular color looks like in person, but as far as your final choice on color if your not %100 sure about color consult an expert cause powder coating is a huge step.
