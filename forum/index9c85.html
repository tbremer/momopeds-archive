I like the "give Justin some beer" part of the plan.


The distance isn't bad; coming from someone who rode 50 miles to the city twice a week. Think about the trip length in the big picture though, as far as how many miles you expect your cylinder/piston to last, etc. If you ported the thing all crazy and expect only 1500 miles out of it (but who would do such a thing?), then 400 miles, or however far it is, would be quite a dent.

Your butt might hate you though; for me, there's a point (about 4 hours) where my butt hurts a lot, but it won't hurt any more, regardless of how long I go after that. (That's what she said). It <b>will</b> be quite a ride.

As far as speed, there's nothing in Missouri to stop you from going flat out the entire way. Even the towns that the country highways go through, they usually don't put stop signs on the county roads, so you don't even have to slow down (for those of us going 30-something).

..weather? Does that matter? If it rains, then you're liquid cooled! Blast mode!

Bryan has done the ride two years that I know of. I went for the second year on my completely stock moped, and I made it there and back. I think the plan was to leave at 9 or 10, and then eat lunch in Lawrence, then be back at a reasonable hour. I doubt we left at 10, but we did still make it to Lawrence at some time close to the lunch hour (2-ish, maybe?) We also made it back well before dark, and it was October then, so it's not like it was getting dark at 9pm anymore. Admittedly, though, that was a somewhat shorter ride than Chuck's place to Lawrence would have been.

Justin, I think that 400 miles is overestimating. Google maps puts it at under 150 miles as "walking directions" one way -- I'm sure those would need some tinkering, but even if you rode round trip, I would think that it would be closer to 300 rather than 400. I guess that's still a big dent if you're only planning for 1500, but then, surely riding to and from the city all the time adds up rather quickly as well...
