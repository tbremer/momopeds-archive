I can service loose bearings in like 15 minutes.  All you need are a couple wrenches and some grease.

That's a lot faster and cheaper than waiting for bearings to show up in the mail (or going to hunt them down in town), dropping $ on them, pressing old ones out, pressing new ones in, hoping the inner spacers are correct, etc.

Plus, I don't think the sealed bearings really stay that much better for much longer than loose bearings.  Too much hype, not enough benefits.

Oh, and if you lose an axle nut your axle slides out in scary ways while riding with sealed bearings...leaving you either stranded 20 miles from home or with a vice grip as an axle nut.  haha
