I asked for a digital copy of the x-rays, but I'm told that nothing is spectacularly broken like a lot of Matt's bones were, so they wouldn't be that impressive. I think they're just letting the pelvis heal on its own, although after they deal with the lack of skin on my ankle, I think I get to have another surgery for them to put pins in there. I also requested that they steal skin from my stomach rather than a thigh or my back (a) because I've already got scars there and nothing they do is really gonna make it worse, and (b) it'll be kind of like a tummy tuck, yay!! TMI, I'm sure.

I got a better look at the rest of me today, and it isn't a pretty picture. I've got road rash all down the right side of my abdomen and on my left side, plus on the right side of my back (that stuck to the sheets, which was really fun to separate). My left arm, which was previously only bruised, has now swelled up like a sausage and was dubbed "the swellbow" by Kenden. I tried to get a few pictures of it all, but I have no way to get them on my computer just yet, so  I've got nothing to show right now. When they open back up my ankle to clean it out tomorrow, I'm gonna see if they can snap a picture for me, because I'm dying to know exactly how bad it looks.

Anyway, as I said earlier, it's gonna be a while before I can ride, let alone walk. So yeah, I'm totally down for injured rider bullshitting at rallies, and I can cook and take care of people and field phone calls and drive the breakdown and all the stuff that can involve sitting on your bum instead of riding.

Anyway, I really appreciate all of you guys. It means a lot to me to know that all of you care, and I am beyond grateful for having such amazing friends.

Yeah Matt, my pelvis break is nothing compared to yours. It feels a little stiff and sore maybe, and I'm not going to be allowed to put weight on it for a while (hence the no walking, since I can't exactly shift all my weight to be on the left ankle), but so far that broken part is the least painful of my broken parts. I think they said there was still a slight chance they'd do surgery on it, but since it was only a little broken and not displaced from its proper position, that it should heal just fine on its own.

Yesterday they gave me a PICC line, which to my understanding, is a really long IV that goes from your arm into one of your arteries really near your heart. It's great because now I don't have IV's hooked up to both hands, AND they don't have to stab me at 4am for blood (seriously, I understand why they do that, but it's the worst idea ever). However, they bandaged that up really well before my arm had started to swell so bad.Then my arm just kept swelling and swelling all day, until we realized that the skin underneath the dressing was irritated and developing gross nasty blisters because of it. They got that all cleaned up though, so it's doing better. Also, the swelling in the "swellbow" has started going down, so I no longer feel like my arm is a boneless, tightly-packed bag of jello.

I'm having issues getting pictures of/getting people to take pictures of my injuries. I guess when I get out of here I'll try to set up a FB album with pics of the moped, my helmet, the clothes they cut off me, and whatever injuries/x-rays I can get decent (but not too grotesque) images of.
