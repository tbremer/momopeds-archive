Title: Re: .... guns
Post by: Garett on February 05, 2009, 06:22:54 pm
Depends on how much you wanna spend too.  Also consider the caliber and how much that cost to shoot.  You don't want to get something that you can't afford to shoot a lot.  I am talking a hundred rounds once a week, to start at least.  Then you can go down to maybe 50 rounds a month/every two weeks or something.  If you have never fired a handgun before, it is very difficult and requires lots and lots of practice if you want to rely on it for anything.

Caliber isn't all that important though, don't get stuck on it. Some people are super into one caliber and they are propaganda nuts about it. Shot placement is where it is at.

9mm is cheap and you can get just about any pistol in it.
.45 is a tried and true low pressure cartridge with a big heavy bullet.
.357 sig or 10mm are your badass rounds in my opinion.

I had a 1911 clone. Its a .45 and they are pretty iconic guns. Its a pretty legendary pistol.

(//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/M1911_Pistol_US.jpg/300px-M1911_Pistol_US.jpg)

Sigs are some of the best guns you can get though.

Go to a range and take a class. You should be able to try out a few guns. You can rent guns too.
