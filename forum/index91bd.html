Post by: Motorcycle Justin on June 11, 2011, 10:42:53 pm
Nope, just basing my facts off the port map (degree timings and time-area make a kit really predictable) and what people have said before.

The TJT, while it will give you a bit higher drive ratio, won't give you much lower drive ratio. The only functional difference is that it's really adjustable. The final range which is basically fixed (say from 5:1-10:1 gear ratio, I don't know what the actual numbers are) really isn't big enough to support really huge exhaust durations. You'll still see low rpm off the line, which can be fixed by clutch springs, but only to a certain extent (I had pretty wild springs on mine and still had issues). Midrange will be good depending on variator weight, and you'll see a higher top speed due to the higher rpm the cylinder is capable of; but the bike as a whole will be less rideable. It all depends on what you're willing to tolerate. But when people see really crazy insane speeds from the kit from the factory, why sacrifice? I've got a TON of real world experience altering port maps. I won't call myself an expert, but I do notice trends. Like my ported stock cylinders, I was starting to get diminishing returns on increases in duration or time-area. Same sort of situation.
