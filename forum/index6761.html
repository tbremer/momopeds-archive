Repair and Performance / Making My Maxi Pedal Crank Longer
« on: July 12, 2011, 03:54:33 pm »
When I was putting my Maxi together I found out that even with a Magnum shaft my pedal arms still would not clear my pipe. I soon found out that I had the wrong pedal arms.

My friend Cody had the right pedal arms since he had a ZA50 pedal shaft, so I gave him my Magnum pedal shaft for his build.

I am not sure what my pedal arms came off of, they are pretty short and straight. I decided I wanted to use them anyways. Cody gave me his ZA50 pedal shaft out of his Maxi and I had my original E50 shaft. So here is what I did:

I put the ZA50 shaft into the frame, and decided how much longer I wanted the shaft. I then cut that amount off of the stock Maxi shaft. Sorry no pictures.

I'm sorry I know not everybody has access to a lathe but I do so I used it. I chucked the stock E50 shaft in the lathe and made sure it was all square then started turning it down:



I turned it down till I had this nipple on the end of the E50 shaft:



I took the ZA50 shaft and pulled the red plastic fitting off of the end. I took the E50 shaft with the nipple on the end and tapped it firmly into the hole in the end of the ZA50 shaft.



When the nipple was made, the E50 shaft also had a beveled edge put in it to make some room for the weld. Here are the 2 shafts welded together:



After the 2 were welded together they were cleaned up some what smooth with a side grinder. I then put the shaft back into the Maxi frame:



I took some nice heavy duty pipe that just slides over the shaft and cut it to length for a spacer. I put the pedal arm back and it now easily clears my pipe:




With my pedal arms being flat they would not clear the ZA50 so I cut the ZA50 shaft off on that side fairly close to the sprocket. I then took a piece of heavy duty pipe and figured out how much room I needed then just welded it all back up a little further out:



It all seems to work pretty good so far.
