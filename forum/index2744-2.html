I have had mega problems running head gaskets in my grand prix.  I also have really high compreshion and the head gasket always fails at the thinest point right next to the decomp hole.  What I have done to fix this is just not run a head gasket.  If you kit is an aluminum cylinder with an aluminum head its actually better in my opinion.  The only trick is that the head and the cylinder have to be perfectly flat. 

To check for flatness, get a piece of glass.  Coat the bottom of your head with oil and then place it on the glass. If you head is flat, the oil will get sqeezed to the edges of the head, if its not it will pool in the high spots. 

To make you head flatter, use the ol' sand paper spray mounted to glass trick.

Pull the pipe off and then check compression. If it drops drastically then you know what the problem is. If you're blowing head gaskets you might consider pulling your timing back a bit too. Finally, running a head gasket is less for sealing (you should have either decked your cyl/head or they were machined flat from the factory) and more for setting appropriate squish. Where's your squish at now? Should be 1-1.4mm. If it's too wide (i.e., if you're running a head gasket and shouldn't be), then all the pre-ignition (not detonation, but I mean before the spark plug sparks) cylinder pressure, instead of being forced into the combustion chamber near the plug, is being blown outward toward the mating surface and head gasket. I've had that happen on my DMP a few times. I solved the problem by machining the cylinder and head to seal to maintain appropriate squish and eliminating the head gasket.
