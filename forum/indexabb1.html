Forced Air Cooled 103
« on: July 31, 2011, 09:04:22 pm »
Its frustrating to have a kitted bike that you never go WOT on because you are in constant feer of overheating.  I love the Grand Prix, but having to look down at the temp gauge every hundred yards really takes the fun out of riding.   Basically the Gila Peugeot kit has fins that are too small to adequately cool itself because the French race boys want their kitted bikes to appear stock.   On top of that when you are blasting the engine variates to a position that blocks air from passing over the fins effectively.  Currently my riding temps are around (360 - 380, 30-35mph)  (380 - 400, 35-40mph)  (400 - 435+, ~ 45 to 50mph)  the bike can go a lot faster but I cant ever open up the throttle because the temp skyrockets in to the danger zone. 

**Attention Justin and all the other tuning gods**  I know I am not the best at high end tuning but I have messed around to the best of my ability with jetting, needles, and timing.  Currently I am running an MVT CDI that starts at 1.3 and has a nice retarded curve.  The needle is on the second position and I am running a 92 main jet.  So lets just say that the bike it tuned as good as it gets for now and I can't dump anymore heat with tuning. 


My Solution!  Take a page from Minarili's and make the 103 forced air-cooled.    I spent some time in the basement this weekend and came up with a mockup.  Its built on  a v1 shroud backing, the small version, which happens to fit perfectly onto the 5 coil 103 stater plate.  I taped the holes of the MVT CDI and mounted a fan from a Marini.   On all that I made a card bord mockup of the potential fan housing which I will make out of sheet metal sometime this week.  Hopefully it can bring the temperatures down 30ish degrees.
