Trouble with my puch polini kit.
« on: October 01, 2012, 10:31:06 pm »
I just replaced my piston on a 64cc polini kit.  Got it all back up and running and took it for a test run around town.  It started and idled with ease.  After running it about 2 miles, it started cutting out at stops unless I held the throttle open a bit.  Took it up to speed and when I came to a light, put on the brakes nice and easy but kicked and died.  I thought I seized but it started up first try and got me home( about 3/4 to a mile) just taking it nice and easy.  Still waiting for it to cool down to get into it, but took out the plug and it is really ashy.  Where should I start?  I  originally had a soft seize.  The cylinder had very minor scoring easily cleaned up with some acid and put back together with a new piston and chrome ring.  I put it back together and dumb ass me put the piston on upside down.  Fixed the mistake after it obviously wouldnt start up.  I was missing a gasket when I put it back together so I made my own with gasket material from the auto shop and am positive I got it right.  Kevin did notice my timing was a bit off when we were trouble shooting the other day and he reset it.  It is also a lot colder tonight than when I was riding this summer with apparently an intake leak.  Could this be as simple as a jet and if so which way should I go.  I am currently running an 86 I believe with a boss pipe and 15mm bing.  Thanks in advance for the pointers.  Im about to get back into it. needs to be cleaned up anyway but could use a list of things to check.

make sure that it has cooled down, them start it up again and spray all  the mating surfaces with carb cleaner. If it bogs down you have an air leak. Seems to me like that is your problem. Otherwise your jet is wayyyy lean, you need to upjet, but really an 86 seems rich. So fix your air leaks fosho then retune. You might want to clean off the plug too, just to get a good reading.

Harry don't give advice on puch polinis if you've never owned one.

With that being said quadruple check your timing with a light.  Make sure its set to 1.0-1.1mm BTDC.  Your jetting is close... polinis love big main jets.   Check for air leaks a lot... make sure everything is sealed.  Did you gap the ring???
