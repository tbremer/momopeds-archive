Who wants to help me with my homework?

It involves mopeds!

Basically, I have to do a mapping project for one of my classes, and we must use the GPS receiver to map things. Because I am interested in environmental justice issues and because I did a project a lot like this last year (and I'm not very creative), I decided that I wanted to map grocery stores in an urban area and then see if there were any patterns ... like, is there less availability to real food (aka not soda, twinkies, and as Mike puts it, "Devil Dicks") in urban environments, or do minorities/poor people have to travel farther to access real food? I still have a lot of background information and definitions to research, but the two areas I need the most help with are determining the scope of the project and actually collecting the points. I fully intend to use mopeds ("because they're fuel efficient") to ride around and collect all the points. Yes, you can just punch the addresses into a thing online and get the points, but the main point of this exercise is to get us to use the GPS receivers. I'm probably going to do both and then compare the accuracy of the online address thing to the points I collected as a second part to the project (because it will take about 5 minutes to do, and talking about it will consume a nice chunk of the number of required pages in the write-up). 

For the scope of the project, I need to know exactly how much area I'm going to cover. It would help if the area was not chosen ENTIRELY arbitrarily, but I'm not going to be too picky so long as there's some order to it. The census data I have is available in tracts, but I'm probably going to sort through grocery store addresses by zip code. So, I've sort of arbitrarily chosen a block of zip codes in KCMO (all in Jackson county), figuring that I'm at least somewhat familiar with some of this area from riding mopeds. In a very cursory glance through a less-than-entirely-accurate online yellow pages service, I came up with approximately 82 stores for this area, but that number will change as I figure out what my final definition of a "grocery store" is. Here's the block that I currently have selected:

Far away version (blue = county, purple = selected zip codes)


Close up version (selected zip codes outlined in bright blue, rail lines shown in brown)


Question A: Does anyone have any advice or input on the area I'm looking at? I'd like to include as much area as possible, but I also want this to be realistic. I can highlight a few roads as reference points if that helps, but I didn't want to label them all because you wouldn't be able to see squat that way.

Question B: Whenever I figure out all the other stuff, does anyone want to come along while I ride around and get points? If nobody else wants to come, I'm totally down for doing it by myself -- I'll probably get lost and break down and it will be a fun learning experience/adventure -- but anyone who wants to brave the cold and who doesn't mind stopping a lot will be welcomed.
