I.Gym.Me, you just let me know how I can help you.
My best advice is to have all the proper Solex tools and be very patient if you are going to do it yourself. Ask questions first if you're not 100% sure of something. Put your hammer in the other room. Don't think of a Solex engine as being the same as other engines...somethings will seem odd and not make sense. Threads are very easily stripped, crankcases are easily cracked, etc. Don't use any gasket sealant anywhere. Take a look at the service manual on my website and see if it looks liek stuff you can do, and if you ahve all the tools shown:
http://velosolex4600.over-blog.com/pages/_Dealer_Training_Manual_October_1975_4600_V2_service_manual-1060111.html
Replacing the crankcase bearing is not a task for the average mechanic. The bearing would not be changed in the old days...a whole new crankcase with the bearing, seal, crankshaft, etc. would be sold/used instead. You won't find instructions in any Solex manual for changing the bearing and/or for some other similar operations.
If the bearing was spinning in the bearing seat of the crankcase, the crankcase is shot.
The connecting rod definitely has to face a particular direction and I have come across several that have been installed backwards and done major damage.
The engine parts can be very expensive. I have very few, if any, good used parts, and I use mostly new parts when reconditioning an engine.
So you'll need to make some decisions as to what you are going to do, and I'm here to help you however I can.
The headlight is extremely difficult to find and very expensive when you do.
Jeff at VeloCruz might have one of the top taillight lenses, also another very difficult item to find.
