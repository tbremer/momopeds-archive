« Reply #13 on: May 27, 2011, 04:08:07 pm »
It really makes me think twice about disregarding tornado warnings/sirens. Like, I think they sound them too often -- the night Reading got destroyed, we had 3-4 tornado warnings for my county, and I think the sirens went off twice in Lawrence. The first tornadic storm was in Topeka and moving northwest, and went nowhere near Lawrence. The second passed about 10 miles south of the southernmost part of Lawrence. We got a few drops of rain the whole night. When the sirens go off for stuff like that, it de-sensitizes us to the danger. The old joke is, "When the tornado sirens go off, we run outside to look" ... although admittedly, if it's raining and hailing and storming like it was in Joplin before the tornado, we're much more likely to take shelter than run outside.

But then you look at those pictures of the destruction, and you're like, "Holy. Fuck." For all that no amount of storm preparation is guaranteed to keep you safe when that kind of tornado hits, it really does drive home the point that everyone needs to take this stuff seriously. I hope that this prompts more businesses to make storm shelters and have emergency storm plans set up, and I hope it prompts more people to take shelter next time a storm is in the area. Maybe a few extra lives can be saved.

Anyway, I'm glad to hear that most of your guys' family and friends are safe, and I'll be keeping my fingers crossed for the ones that are still incommunicado.
