It was Jimmy, okay!  I learned it by watching Jimmy!! 

Seriously, Jimmy and Tracy live right by me, and Jimmy kept stopping by when I was working on my 1967 Volvo Wagon and my 1968 Sedan.  First Jimmy came by in his Vegi-Diesel Mercedes, they he started coming by on Mopeds.  Then a guy selling a vintage Volvo listed a 1978 Vespa Bravo for sale.  Got it for $50.00 and $300 later it was complete and running.  I got the Bravo home the same day as the IRE's Spring 2008 Rally.. (maybe 2009... it's getting blurry). 

I finally met up with some of you guys for the April 2009 Macklind Days event.  Since then, I've been hanging on your fringes, gradually building up my Vespa knowledge and bike collection. 

--
Oh yeah, motivation is largely economical transport and more of the joy of being able to fix my transport.  The need for speed is to get through the ghetto between here and my work... But they are a hell of a lot of fun to ride.
