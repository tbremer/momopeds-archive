So.... hotels in Chicago suck, apparently. The first one I called (Lugo Hotel) only goes by the week/month. Next I called the Chinatown one, went through their phone system, finally got ahold of a real person (who didn't speak English well), and promptly got disconnected. So yeah, I'm gonna go with "no" on that one. After that, I kinda gave up on calling people.

The other two choices I had were the Amber Inn, and the downtown Travelodge. Amber Inn looks to be the cheapest, and parking would probably be less of a bitch. However, the site I was looking at says they only had one room left for that weekend. Maybe the story would be different if I called, but either way, it's a smaller hotel, and the chances that they could take all of us are a lot smaller.

There are lots of the big-name hotels downtown. The Mariott was $169 a night $30/day parking, and you could have up to 5 people. I saw nothing about an "extra person" charge. The Holiday Inn was $199/night + $36/day parking, up to 5 people, but the 3rd, 4th, and 5th people were $15 extra each. La Quinta was $179 a night, with no info about max. people, parking charges, or extra person charges. Travelodge is $179/night for a 2-double-bed-and-kitchenette room (if you say you have 4 people), or $159/night if you say you've only got two people in the room, plus $33/day parking.

TL;DR

Assuming the prices listed on all these websites are correct, my current vote is for the Marriott (625 South Ashland Avenue at Harrison Street/Chicago, IL 60607). It's the cheapest big hotel, and it's MARGINALLY closer to rally happenings than the others (although it's still a good 1.5-2 miles away), and the route to the given intersection looks fairly straightfoward. Parking will be an issue, especially if we're bringing trailers. Honestly, I'm not even sure they can accommodate trailers, or if they can, we'll probably be charged for two parking spaces. So yeah... I'll discuss this more with CT's. If we reserve a room somewhere, I'll post up, and if any of you find a better deal or bite the bullet and make a reservation somewhere, let the rest of us know.

If you bring peds and park them, they need to be in a secure locked trailer. (like what Rickems brought last year)
