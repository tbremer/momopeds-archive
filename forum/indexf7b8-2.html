Repair and Performance / Re: Airsal 50 anyone?
« on: October 14, 2011, 06:41:59 am »
Yeah, I had the Treats 50 kit, not the Airsal, but they're pretty similar. It did a solid 40 just bolted on, as Mike said I reproduced the 70cc port map on it and went 45-ish. I kept massaging my port map and finally ended up with a solid 50/50 - 19PHBG and Boss. I put a 6p on it a couple times and went well over 50 - probably 54 or so; but with a serious midrange scavenge issue that made it difficult to get past 30 in certain situations (it would have been perfect on a ZA). They're solid kits if you're willing to mess with them a little. I'd say the Airsal would be a little more manageable compared to the Treats 50 - if only because of the boost ports. You could really throw a lot of blowdown at it and still be okay, especially on a ZA.

General Discussion / Re: Looking for Justin
« on: August 13, 2011, 07:38:25 pm »
Hey, if you want port info, look on MA; the P4 port map is a good place to start. It gets better with more port area and higher blowdown values (hint, hint). You can port a PA50I cylinder to these specifications if you're crafty, but it's easier to start with a PA50II cyl so you don't have to mess with transfers so much. I'm out of the porting game so you'll have to work with it yourself, lotsa guys out your way full of knowledge though.

Other than porting, get a good pipe (anything but a Proma), get a 12mm Keihin and jet it right, get carbon reeds (.4mm works well) on a PA50II block, notch your variator and run PA50I weights on a PA50II ramp plate. Get a new belt: Gates AX40. Time it so it runs, my Hobbit never gave a shit about point gap. And for god's sake get new tires and brakes (shoes, cables); can't go fast if you can't stop fast.

Good luck and have fun.

PS: ^ That's the formula for the most hilarious stock Hobbit you'll ever ride. Mine (including all customer cylinders) were doing 45+ with KILLER low end and great mpg.
