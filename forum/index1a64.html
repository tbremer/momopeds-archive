I suppose the idea of doing this is to increase the surface area of the combustion chamber and let the air fuel mix fully spread about it, but in order for it to work, you'd have to mess with the timing, and either do one of 3 things to make up for the added surface area, otherwise the air fuel mix would either stay in the chamber too long, or not long enough...
1: Swap out cranks for one with longer crank journals for more piston movement in the cylinder (which would allow more fuel to enter the cylinder, as well as compress the mixture more than a stock crank)
2: Find longer con-rods to compress the mix more, but allow less to enter in the chamber
3: Find a high comp head, or mill down the existing one in order to equal the amount of surface area in the chamber before the 'upgrade'

IMO I feel like it would be way better to just cough up some dough and buy a race crank, and a high comp head. It'll do the same thing, without damaging existing parts

the way it looks to me (based on browsing other forums with searches) is that the dimples cause the airflow (pre, mid, and post combustion) to not "stick" to the smooth surfaces of the piston and head where they normally have deposits form. much like the dimples on a golf ball promote a cushion of air that actually makes the ball fly further, these dimples seem to promote a smoother burn and less deposits. Keeps the fuel mix from collecting anywhere? I'm not totally sure.

From a mopar forum:
Quote
On the Dimpling shown, I can only speculate on its possible advantages or disadvantages.

One fact I can state is that a dimpled surface will allow air to flow more smoothly over it.

On the head where its dimpled it may slightly improve the intake charge flow.

personally? if someone wants to try it, go for it. If i had the parts to throw away just in case? yeah, I'd try it. but you'd probably only see changes in a perfectly tuned machine, which (we all know) I'm not up to, yet.
