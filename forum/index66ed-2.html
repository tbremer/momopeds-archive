      Being nice is not what this is about, its getting to the bottom of why this is occurring. The answers are sometimes not nice.
      Being out of round is very unlikely but can be measured with telescopic guages and a micrometer. A visual clue would be that the crosshatching is worn off of only two opposing sides of the bore. This also unlikely to produce a seize.
      With the presence of the proper amount of lubricant the niksil cannot bond to the steel of the ring. The temperature of the ring exceeded parameters through timing, air/fuel mix, or friction issues. Timing is unlikely in that many e50 are run at full advance without seize. Oil/Fuel Ratio or Air/Fuel Ratio are the suspects here.
