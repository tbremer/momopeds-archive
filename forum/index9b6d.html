Local Discussion (MidMopeds) / Exoctic Missouri: a mid-Mopeds tour of the Mid-Mo
« on: November 09, 2009, 11:51:16 am »
Here are some ideas on rides, just ideas.  Add to it, talk shit about the routes or locations, and/or post other spots.

Harrisburg:
Route E (fastest route):  http://maps.google.com/maps?hl=en&resnum=0&q=Boonville+mo&um=1&ie=UTF-8&hq=&hnear=Boonville,+MO&gl=us&ei=vED4SvXHD5PSMsr_8egF&sa=X&oi=geocode_result&ct=image&resnum=1&ved=0CAwQ8gEwAA

Route J and Route E (Loop Route...take the fast route back)
http://maps.google.com/maps?hl=en&resnum=0&q=Boonville+mo&um=1&ie=UTF-8&hq=&hnear=Boonville,+MO&gl=us&ei=vED4SvXHD5PSMsr_8egF&sa=X&oi=geocode_result&ct=image&resnum=1&ved=0CAwQ8gEwAA

Route VV/124 to Route J (Better Loop Route)
http://maps.google.com/maps?hl=en&resnum=0&q=Boonville+mo&um=1&ie=UTF-8&hq=&hnear=Boonville,+MO&gl=us&ei=vED4SvXHD5PSMsr_8egF&sa=X&oi=geocode_result&ct=image&resnum=1&ved=0CAwQ8gEwAA

Harrisburg does not offer much to do however the best BBQ around here can be found at the four way stop sign (where Route J and Hwy 124 meet up)  It would be a good ride during the week as I am unsure of their hours on weekends.  Once they run out of something they don't make more until the next day.

I call one a better route because Route E can be kinda a bitch as can Hwy 124 but you would be on 124 for much less time where speeds are much less (in theory)  Route VV does have alot of traffic but also a pretty wide shoulder in the early part, then traffic kinda heavy but drops off quick.  Red Rock Rd, Route YY and Route J are pretty rides, With J having the most traffic.

Fayette:
http://maps.google.com/maps?hl=en&resnum=0&q=Boonville+mo&um=1&ie=UTF-8&hq=&hnear=Boonville,+MO&gl=us&ei=vED4SvXHD5PSMsr_8egF&sa=X&oi=geocode_result&ct=image&resnum=1&ved=0CAwQ8gEwAA

There may not be alot to do here either, but there is a college in town so maybe there is at least a chance you could get a drink.  Hwy 240 moves alot of traffic and is pretty busy in parts with no shoulder and traffic moves real fast for the speed limit (55).  There is a scrap yard on the way up there I don't know if they have anything for mopes or not.

Boonville:
http://maps.google.com/maps?hl=en&resnum=0&q=Boonville+mo&um=1&ie=UTF-8&hq=&hnear=Boonville,+MO&gl=us&ei=vED4SvXHD5PSMsr_8egF&sa=X&oi=geocode_result&ct=image&resnum=1&ved=0CAwQ8gEwAA

The Hwy 40 route allows for about 6 miles of straight flood plain riding....you can blast as fast as you want with little traffic.  There is no shoulder but because of the low traffic volume cars can pass using the other lane...maybe

Moberly:
Fuck it, they have a drive in...and on a 'ped.... what more could you ask for?  Other then sound

Jefferson City:
http://maps.google.com/maps?hl=en&resnum=0&q=Boonville+mo&um=1&ie=UTF-8&hq=&hnear=Boonville,+MO&gl=us&ei=vED4SvXHD5PSMsr_8egF&sa=X&oi=geocode_result&ct=image&resnum=1&ved=0CAwQ8gEwAA

The Mo. River bridge may be an issue, if you go make sure you get off on the first exit after the bridge as the shoulder/bike lane disappears and it gets real tight going into the 54/50 interchange.  While your in the capitol city, you can go ice skating, get ice cream at Central dairy, check out the capitol grounds on 'peds and take a Christmas picture on the capitol lawn!  Be sure to tell the Gov. hello.

Fulton Mo:
http://maps.google.com/maps?hl=en&resnum=0&q=Boonville+mo&um=1&ie=UTF-8&hq=&hnear=Boonville,+MO&gl=us&ei=vED4SvXHD5PSMsr_8egF&sa=X&oi=geocode_result&ct=image&resnum=1&ved=0CAwQ8gEwAA

The route I put here skips going out Broadway (Hwy WW) because that is a pretty dangours road, one of the most fatal in Boone County.  The Ashland route along route MM has big hills and is well traveled however far less then WW.  This Trip takes you by Ashland Lake (about 4 miles east of Ashland) there is an abandoned Univ. of Mo. field house there...I would stop there for a meeting on safety, it over looks the lake at the damn side, its really a pretty place to stop.  Also, you would be riding mainly through the Mark Twain National Forest for alot of the ride, there are places to pull off and meet up if you want.

Once in Fulton, there are bars and such but the Churchill Memorial is a gem of the midwest, worth checking out...its where he gave his Iron Curtain speech, there is one large section of the wall there as well as a church and a really nice, short, quick museum you can tour....20 years ago TODAY (Nov 9) the Berlin Wall fell

Sedalia mo:
http://maps.google.com/maps?hl=en&resnum=0&q=Boonville+mo&um=1&ie=UTF-8&hq=&hnear=Boonville,+MO&gl=us&ei=vED4SvXHD5PSMsr_8egF&sa=X&oi=geocode_result&ct=image&resnum=1&ved=0CAwQ8gEwAA

Home of the State Fair, Scott Joplin, and twice a day Amtrack service,  Sedalia has a soft spot in my heart.  There are the usuall bars and such but also on the west side of the SDL there is the Daum Museum of Contemporary Art, the New York Times called it one of the best museums in the Midwest, its free to go and they have some pretty cool stuff (a HUGE blown glass piece, its like 12-15ft high).  If you want to go here, let me know I can tell you more about the Queen City then you ever wanted to know.

Once you get to SDL you may as well go on to Warrensburg it would be a good overnight trip if you wanted to camp someplace or get four or five folks in a motel room.
