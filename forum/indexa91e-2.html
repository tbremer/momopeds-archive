I'm just now coming up on being into mopeds for a year. My first was an F. Morini bike. I found it in a shed, and was told that "if you can make it run, you can have it". I wanted to make it fast from the first time it ran. Didn't know a thing about anything, though. I started reading on MA, and got a pipe for it in January. It went about 35, which never really was enough. Looking back, there were a million things I could've done to make it fast, but just didn't. It's now in pieces in our basement shop, Kevin bought the motor about two months ago. After I started seriously looking for a Puch, Nash (A-ko) posted up on MA about having a silver Maxi for sale, and that he was coming through KC. He showed up in March, I think, in a freezing cold parking lot in the middle of downtown KC. It wasn't even running. We hobbled it together enough to get it running, I test-rode it for half a block, and bought it on the spot. Stock (2hp derestricted), it was heaps faster than my Morini bike. Got a pipe for it soon after, then I kitted it right before I started riding with Linda, Ed, and the BM. I remember my first group ride like it was yesterday. I laid the Maxi over in a dirty left-hander, the night sky was filled with an amazing light(ning) show, filled up three (!) times, I think, and made the best friends I've had for a long, long time. Thanks, mopeds.

Freshman year of college, I really wanted a "moped" (scooter) because I was too lazy to walk and too impatient to wait for the bus. I halfheartedly looked all year, except I was too cheap to buy one of the $800 POS scooters (this is ironic now, considering how much I've spent on mopeds). In May-ish, I found a guy selling a Honda Express for $500. I ended up not buying it because the mechanic I took it to told me that the starter spring could break, and there was pretty much no way to repair it. Once I figured out that there were older (and significantly cheaper) alternatives to the cheap scooters, though, I went searching. I found Moped Army, posted in the buy/sell looking for a moped nearby, and ended up buying the Swinger/Kromag from Bryan a few months later (that was August 07, I believe). There was a rather steep learning curve for a while -- when I first got the moped, I had no idea what a spark plug was, what it did, or how you might remove one. I was too afraid to take anything apart unless I absolutely had to, so I contented myself with cleaning it. A lot. That October, I saw an ad for a $100 Tomos on CL, and I  bought it so I could take things apart and learn about them without messing up my good moped. I did that all winter, and all spring. I went to Kzoo that May (2008), and could not wipe the smile off my face for weeks afterwards, despite learning at the rally that stock mopeds weren't quite as cool as I'd thought. The performance upgrades began after that rally. Then in July, I left for 5 months to go abroad, and came back in December absurdly eager to get back to work on the mopeds. I got the Free Spirit in January (2009), spent 6 months tearing it apart and putting it back together, then blew it up within two weeks of getting it running. I also got the parts Tomos in there somewhere, and that now runs.

My motivations are education and pleasure. Mopeds are fun to ride, but it turns out, they're also fun to work on. I NEVER would have thought I would know everything that I now know about how mopeds work, but I'm glad I do. Thanks to the forums, and to everyone who took the time to explain things until I understood them, I don't have to be dependent on someone else to fix my shit when it breaks, and that feeling is truly priceless. There's still heaps more to know, but I figure that if I hear about something I don't understand enough times, and I want to understand it badly enough, eventually I will understand it. That theory has worked so far, at least.

Also, it turns out that mopeds have this strange power to attract some damn cool people who I never would have met if I didn't also have a moped. You all are a source of information, a source of inspiration, my friends, and almost like a second family. I have no idea who I would be or what I would do with myself if I didn't have mopeds and the people I've met thanks to mopeds, but looking back, I can definitely say that getting a moped was one of the best decisions I ever made.
