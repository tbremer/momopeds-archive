14/45 is a little low gearing-wise. Unless you've got some gnarly hills to climb, you could bump that front sprocket up to a 16 tooth, and that would probably help. When I switched mine, I didn't notice much loss on the low end (although honestly I didn't notice much improvement on top either), but it just felt more powerful all around. Maybe that's just be not noticing things properly though, because gearing is one of the few things that ought to be a pretty cut-and-dry mod.

Do the little stuff too, like make sure your tires are aired up,your chain isn't too loose or tight, your brakes aren't rubbing, and the tires aren't rubbing the fenders or swingarm. Make sure you don't have any air leaks. 64 sounds about right for that setup, but maybe mess around with the jetting and/or needle settings a little just to see if you can find any improvements that way. Matching the outside of your exhaust port to the pipe will help a little (that was another one of those things that wasn't so much about mph as it was about making it run smoother and better, in my experience). If you get inspired, also mess with your timing. The long version on how to do that is here: //www.mopedarmy.com/wiki/Ignition_timing_for_points or the short version is here: //www.youtube.com/watch?v=eoi82ftK1nE (I wouldn't use that video for a kitted bike, but on a stock cylinder, it should be fine.)

Buy or otherwise get your hands on a few jets in either direction.  Without riding it, no one is going to be able to tell you over the internet if you are rich, lean, or dead on.  You might as well get used to learning the difference firsthand now.  I'm sure anybody here that has tuned a bike or two would be more than willing to give you a hand, but it really is in your best interest to start right now with a stock bike and learn to tune a carb by sheer trial and error completely by yourself.  Think of it like teaching yourself how to catch a fish.  If nothing else, if you want to be able to do the funner things you can do to a bike like kitting and putting a huge pipe or carb or cdi ignition or all three and you don't learn how to tune your carb, you will be lost when you are only hitting 40mph on your built up bike.
