Hey guys, so my hobbit's not variating all the way. 

I'd like to get into the back driven pulley to clean it up and get it spreading apart a little easier.  I'm curious if there are any tricks to getting it off without the pulley puller.
I saw Pavel's method but it says to hold the gearbox and all that but it's still not really budging.  Any home-methods using an improvised pulley puller?

Also, I'm curious about the spring that's in my front variator.  It doesn't show up in this pdf (//www.mopedarmy.com/mediawiki/images/f/f0/Honda_PA50_Parts.pdf ).  So I'm wondering if it belongs in there at all.

the spring in there is between parts 2 and 6 of the variator (front) blow-out.  Since it's not listed, I guess it shouldn't be there and is what may be causing my variator to not variate all the way?  Either way I know I have to get into the rear drive assembly. 

Tricks, tips, or do you have a honda pulley puller tool I can use?

there isn't a spring in the (front) variator.  there are starter clutch springs but that wouldn't have any affect on how fully you variated.  what i've seen/and done before is get a can of teflon spray lubricant and spray into the pulley area (where the spring is) and gently squeeze the belt together to force the pulley cheeks open.  do that a few times and it should start opening all of the way while it's running.
