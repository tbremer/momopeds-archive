Title: Bravo!
Post by: VolvoManJack on October 18, 2010, 08:23:12 pm
Mark got those carb bushings from ZippyMoped... but no matter. 

Owning two 1978 bravos myself (and only one is running right now :( ) I'll suggest a few things that I've learned the hard way 

1) Plug fouling kills the performance more than anything.  I learned this the hard way.  Even stock setups get fsckd. Pull it, clean it, replace it.  It does wonders...  Poor engine performance is just as sluggish as a slipping belt.  You can't necessarily that the engine has low power tell until it is under load. 

2) Stock belts (w/o teeth) get glazed and start slipping under load - especially noticeable when you try to start the bike. 

3) I've greased the shit out of my variators and it does very little to change performance. 

4) if the variator side plates are moving (independent of the main cylinder), you will lose power.  If the rear clutch pads are oily or wearing out, you will also lose performance. 

5) Partial gas starvation will also kill performance (yeah duh right?). 

So... I recommend you determine if the engine is low-powered, or the drivetrain is not working correctly.  Clean everything with break cleaner and try it yourself. 

That's my Nickle.
