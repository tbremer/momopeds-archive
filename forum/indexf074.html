Yup I remember you saying that but, you now have leaky seals correct? Higher viscosoty fluids will build more case preasure, to much preasure and you will start blowing your tranny fluid past the crank seal. From what I have read on MA and seen through this forum, 12oz is a good safe fill point. Gives you some slip to help you hit second about 20, also gives enough fluid to hit that crank bearing ;-) The seals and clutches in this new one look PRISTINE!

I will probably go up to 12oz of Type F in this one to help offset what the porting will take from the bottom end.

I have avoided the clutch flip in both the ZAs due to the total pain in the ass of removing them to replace the ones you're going to blow up. Changing the pucks is not that bad! A one time deal if you take your time and do it right.
