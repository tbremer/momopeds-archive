Hey Jack,

I am sorry to hear such a young child had to stumble upon that type of activity, that is nothing anyone should have to stumble upon and to have been a young child makes it even more wrenching.

More along the lines of public information, the the City of Columbia does post all of their emergency responses for the Fire department and ambulance calls in the city (in real time and archived), additionally they post most police calls after 24 hours, given the delay they are all archived and edited to some degree. Here is the link that will direct you to either:

http://gocolumbiamo.com/PSJC/Services/911/911dispatch/

After looking over the calls for fire and EMS in the city, I would imagine the CAD# for the call you are talking about is: 201023558 it may also be known as 23558.  The address listed for that call is 1728 East Broadway, putting it in the area of Old 63 and Broadway however not the closest cross street so this may not be correct.

As for media coverage, usually local outlets give little attention to incidents that appear to have been self inflicted. I don't know anything about this incident but I would be surprised if anything were to be reported given then nature of the incident. 

You, me, your mother, the parents of the child or the child herself could contact the Columbia Police Department and have the officer assigned to that beat make contact with them in order to have a conversation about the events.  The officer will be part of the Southeast Patrol Division, contact info can be found on this page:  http://www.gocolumbiamo.com/Police/Divisions/SoutheastPatrolDivision.php         I would bet Sgt. Will Wilson will be  your best bet for info about this incident.

I don't know much but I figured this info may be a start in what your looking for.  Let me know if you have any questions or need anything else.  I am sorry to hear someone you hold dear had to be involved in it and wish them my best getting beyond this.
