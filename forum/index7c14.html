Tuning the Moby
« on: August 02, 2011, 10:20:36 am »
Picked up old Copper from Phil, and she needs just a bit of work!  Seriously, the least amount of work I have needed to do on a ped   A few nuts and bolts here and there, and some tuning, and we will be slick! 

She has a 14/12 Dell on it, which seems to be stock.  I spent a lot of time messing with this on my tomos, so I am totally familiar with it.  Plus it is easy to get to! 

Needs a better exhaust.  Have one ordered. 

Right now she has no bottom end.  When the clutch starts to grab the engine wants to bog down a lot.  Above 5k rpm it opens up and pulls clean to 30mph no problem. 

So now I am on the quest for tuning the bottom end.  I am sure the pipe will help a lot, but I want to do what I can right now.  I have never had a ride that had no low end, but still had top end power.  Normally is just crappy all around when it won't pull a hill from a stop. 


The plans:

clean the carb (keep it, or maybe replace it?)
ensure air leaks are taken care of
install exhaust flange and awesome exhaust
try different jets
Get fancy variator or remove two balls from stock one

I'm sure there are plenty of air leaks at the exhaust right now, since the cylinder mount is stripped like all moby's seem to be.  What else could cause lack of low end but plenty of top end?


And the quest begins.

Re: Tuning the Moby
« Reply #6 on: August 02, 2011, 11:27:43 am »
There are timing marks on the flywheel that shows when the CDI fires.  You have to pull the flywheel, set the piston to 1.2-1.5mm BTDC and line up the firing marks, tap the flywheel back on, and then it should be good to go.  DON'T FORGET IT'S REVERSE THREADS TO LOOSEN THE FLYWHEEL NUT!! Ryan Nash has a Buzetti ignition micrometer or you can use a sliding micrometer thingy.

Some CDI info:
http://www.mopedarmy.com/wiki/MBK_CDI
