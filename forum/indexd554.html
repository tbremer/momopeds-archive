Yep, what it says. I think I've had enough with what I've got; I'm ready to move on to (one of) the science project(s), methinks. As far as mileage, I've never had a odometer that's worked long enough to really give me any sort of exact number, so it's estimated. Shipping isn't included; ten bucks will get either of em to ya. Just kick me an email. The K-star is available immediately, the DMP will be available after the Mid-Mo rally. I won't blow it up, promise.

38mm K-Star- $50; $60 w/head
Port matched to a 28mm header, and a 21mm ROUND intake.
Altered port map, ask me for details. A lot of work and maths. Revs to 10.5k-ish with a Boss, 11k+ with a 6p
This cylinder GPS averaged 50 exactly (49 up slight hill, 51 down) with me on it, with the Boss. It's quite a bit faster with the 6p.
Comparable-to-stock low end with 16x45 gearing. That said, you need to know how to tune a clutch; obviously. It's a 50/50.
Like Linda said, "It just keeps going"
Probably right around 1000 miles on it; most of it was riding to KC and back at 40 or so. You can still see the hone marks, normal shiny wear on the high spots of the piston. Ring is good. Not a lot of compression with that head, it's the normal hi-comp, not the hi-hi comp.

45mm DMP - $70 - Head not included
Port matched to a 28mm header, and 21mm ROUND intake.
Altered port map. Lots of time and maths in this one too. It rocks. Seriously. I can also send you a port map for this one, if you really want it. Also revs to 11k+ with the 6p.
This cylinder may have touched 60, as indicated by my flaky bicycle computer; but I was passing cars on a 55-mph road. It'll do a solid 50 no matter what, uphill, wind, whatever.
Good low end, especially with clutch tuning.
I was up front with the master blasters in Chicago; got compliments for how stupid fast it was for a DMP.
Probably 600 miles on this one; it was sitting at 200 miles in Chicago, 500 last week, and I rode to the city a few times since then. So yeah, 600-something.
It's worn really well for being so fast, just the shiny high spots, no scoring, all smoooooth. New piston ring! You'll get the old one too. It's good, just wanted to replace it. Stupid amounts of compression with a 70cc HC head.
