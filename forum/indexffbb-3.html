that tool is called a tungsten carbide burr, I have gone through severall.

they are not "rare", nor "expensive", a good one should run about $25 and last all season.

Lindar you have seen me using this thing on many occasions (porting intakes and jugs on the back porch?), they are very handy.

needs to be turned at least 30,000 RPM, so get the biggest air tank you can afford (or a twin cylinder compressor).

1/4" standard shank for air tools

this thing EATS cast iron, and aluminum alive. so be careful.

you might consider using a cordless drill at first until you get used to it, or if your work peace is expensive.
