Those studs with nuts in the center are just to space the pad closer to the bell.  Less travel = less vibration and chaos in your engine.

The screws and washers on the end of the arms can be used to add or remove weight.  So it's not just tuned, it's further tunable.

The studs with circlips have been drilled and tapped for a retaining plate.

you guys are over thinking it, just make sure all your shoes weigh exactly the same, then with the clutch mounted in some completely stationary fixture, use a precise fish scale to pull on the clutch shoe until it opens. record the scale reading of your fish scale and repeat for each shoe, always hooking the scale to the same spot on every shoe just to be consistent. Adjust your spring tension until each shoe opens at the same amount of force being pulled on. This will be a relatively easy way of making sure they all open at the same time. selecting your rpm is a trial process by increasing the amount of tension using the same method.
