//www.treatland.tv/minarelli-V1-bennasar-80cc-turbo-p/bennasar-minarelli-052.htm
Yeah, just get a pipe first. ^Probably that one. Get used to rejetting your carb and setting your timing and all that.

You could get a carb next, but most likely the bigger carb you'd want to use with your kit, won't work so very well with your stock cylinder. So probably just get the kit and carb at the same time. Read up on Moped Army about that 80cc Polini, there are some things you might have to do to get it to work with your V1, like cutting the piston skirt for crank clearance. But I don't know.

On a two stroke you DON'T want to have a bigger cylinder with stock carb/exhaust. You'll have loads of performance issues, it'll probably overheat, bad times all around. And unlike your four-stroke, you can usually get a decent amount of performance out of the stock cylinder just by porting it and putting a pipe on it.

It means that it's designed for 80cc's. It'll work okay on bigger or smaller displacements. On a smaller cylinder, it'll hit a LOT harder and later than it would on an 80 (probably wouldn't hit at all on a stock cylinder). Thing is, on bikes like Puch, there are pipes that will work on a stock cylinder AND on a kit (Boss, Estoril, Circuit), but as far as I can tell, most Minarelli pipes are for kitted bikes, having huge headers, and big chambers.

I'd stay away from ordering online from 1977, especially on an item that's "special order". Email Benji and ask when he'll have Bennasar pipes back in stock.

You could totally also hack and weld a Puch/Tomos/whatever pipe to work with your bike.
