Garelli Clutch Cover Paintjob

After working on this damn clutch for 4 months, I decided it was time to give the thing a pretty paint job. The rest of the bike isn't in too great of shape (yet) but since Garelli clutches are notorious for problems it seemed like a good place to start.
Don't forget to save that used ND30 Oil for your stir fry.


Here's the clutch (use rivets, for your health)


Step 1: Remove shitty paint, wear goggles and drunk


Step 2: Sand, sand primer primer primer sober


Step 3: Paint 4 coats (used a white paint pen for the letters) and get all them inhalant particles YUM


Step 4: A wild Fruit Stripe has appeared! Level Up!


Step 5: (Not pictured) Clear coat sexy and let it sit for a week
