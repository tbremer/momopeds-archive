The stroke is slightly off from a batavus to a Puch.  Tom investigated this possibility, and if I remember what he said, you need to press a puch conrod into the batavus crank to make it work.

So, you need this, and a machine shop:
http://www.treatland.tv/puch-moped-con-rod-kit-crankshaft-p/puch-crankshaft-con-rod-kit.htm

Also, the kit is slightly cheaper from treats:
http://www.treatland.tv/puch-moped-65cc-metrakit-cylinder-kit-p/puch-metrakit-65cc-cylinder.htm

I've used this kit on a puch for over a year and can vouch for it's greatness.  Power and reliability, which is a rare combo with kitted bikes.  Pair it with a 19mm phbg carb and a pipe of your choice (I prefer the techno boss), and you've got a solid bike.

Batavus with this is all experimental though.  Do it!
