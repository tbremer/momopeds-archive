I feel like we might be overthinking this.

Is that a beautiful bike? Yes. Is it fast? Hell if I know; I don't have fast mopeds. It certainly left an impression on those of you who rode with it, though.

Some people like top speed and don't mind sacrificing some low end for it. Some people like torque. Both are fun and both have their merits. I'm sure that it suits his purposes just fine, and if it didn't, he is perfectly capable of changing it.

Would I, or would any of you really buy that moped, even if you had $1600 stashed away under your mattress and no other financial obligations? I wouldn't. If I had that kind of money laying around, I'd throw it at making my own moped awesome. I probably couldn't achieve the same level of win that that moped has, but it would be far more satisfying to build up my own moped than to buy one that was already that nice. Not to be clich?d, but I'm more interested in the journey than the destination.

Also, there is a risk in purchasing ANYTHING mechanical that it will stop working. Most places will provide a warranty in case this happens, although that usually isn't the case with mopeds given their contrary nature. I would imagine he has that tuned and broken in well enough that it shouldn't break down or seize, but that doesn't stop the random air leak from happening, or from it being ridden too hard in the cold, or from someone messing up the oil mix, or whatever else. It's a moped. It works now. It will work for some time. It will break eventually. How soon that "eventually" is depends on a lot of factors, but we wouldn't be adding performance enhancements to our mopeds if the risk wasn't worth the reward.
